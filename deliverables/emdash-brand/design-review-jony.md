# Design Review: Emdash Brand Identity

**Reviewer:** Jony Ive, Visual Design Director  
**Date:** April 2, 2026  
**Status:** Strong foundation with one refinement needed

---

## The Verdict

This is thoughtful work. The logo is honest—a horizontal bar is exactly what an em dash is. The wordmark in Inter feels right for infrastructure. But there's a tension worth resolving.

### 1. Logo Strength

Yes, the bar stands alone. It's minimal enough to scale down to favicon size without losing meaning. There's confidence in that simplicity. The proportions matter, though—slightly rounded ends prevent it from feeling sharp or bureaucratic. This will work everywhere: mark-only, full logo, dark, light.

### 2. Indigo as Primary

It works. Indigo reads as precision rather than energy, which is correct for a developer tool. It avoids the clichés (Vercel blue, GitHub's tones). The hover state (#4F46E5) provides enough contrast without needing a third variant. My only concern: in the light palette (Paper background), ensure the indigo-light backgrounds (#EEF2FF) pair with the indigo-dark text (#3730A3) to maintain readability at small sizes.

### 3. Typography: Inter 600

Correct choice. Inter at semibold weight says "designed but not designed." It's engineering-grade without coldness. Tight tracking (-0.02em) helps—it prevents the wordmark from feeling loose. Good.

### 4. Accessibility & Contrast

The palette passes WCAG AA for all combinations listed. One thing to verify when you apply it: test the slate borders (#64748B) against Paper (#FAFAFA) backgrounds—that's approximately 6:1 contrast, which is fine, but watch it at 14px. The indigo on Paper is strong enough. Status colors are fine.

### 5. One Suggestion

The accent color is Indigo. But the *mark*—the horizontal bar—should always be treated as a fourth neutral, not painted in Indigo. Let it be Ink on light backgrounds, Paper on dark. The wordmark can be indigo sometimes (when it's a call to action), but the mark itself should be structurally independent. It's the separator. It shouldn't advocate.
