# Pricing Review: Emdash Credit System
**Reviewer:** Sara Blakely, Growth Strategist

---

## My Assessment

**1. Plan Positioning ($29/$79/$199)**
Strong. These hit the sweet spot between too cheap (no perceived value) and too expensive (blocks adoption). $29 feels accessible for freelancers testing the platform. The 3x jump to $79 rewards power users without being a shock. Good ladder.

**2. Credit Allocation**
Smart trade-off. 10 credits for a full site feels earned—clients see the scope. Content revisions at 1 credit is genius: it encourages iteration without guilt. The ratio says "we trust you to build here."

**3. "Never Block Work" Policy**
This is your moat. It removes friction and builds trust. **But watch for it:** Some clients will intentionally run overages thinking it's unlimited. You'll need clear communication about overage rates ($3/credit at Starter) so they're not surprised.

**4. One-Month Rollover**
Generous enough. It rewards light months without creating hoarding mentality. However, it leaves money on the table—consider 6-week rollover instead. Clients feel less rushed, and you capture more overages.

**5. One Change for Higher Conversions**
Offer a **"try free" entry point**: 5 credits for free (one small site or 5 revisions). No payment method required. Let them feel the product before committing. This is how Spanx won retail shelf space—let people touch it first.

---

**Overall:** This system trusts the customer. That's rare and powerful. Ship it.

—Sara
