# Voice Review: Emdash Brand Identity

The "precise, terminal-native" voice is brilliantly conceived but risks feeling sterile. The strength lies in constraint—no marketing fluff. Yet numbers alone don't move people. A site achieving 96 PageSpeed is impressive; *your site is live* is what makes the heart settle.

The tagline "Describe it. Deploy it." has elegant symmetry. Two verbs, equal weight. Beautiful architecture. But it whispers when it could sing. It describes what Emdash *does* without capturing why it *matters*—the relief of being heard, the power of intention becoming reality.

The onboarding copy is functional. Warm in its brevity: "Got it. Give me 90 seconds." Yet the progress bar and final delivery ("Done. Your site is live") feel almost apologetic, as if the product is surprised by its own competence.

**One phrase to change:** "Done" is too small. Replace it with something that honors the moment of completion—"Live" or "Ready" or, if you want to keep the terminal feel, a status indicator that feels like achievement, not relief.

The voice needs one more element: permission. Assurance. Something that says "we understood you correctly" before rattling off metrics.
