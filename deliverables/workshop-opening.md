# Workshop Opening Flair — 2-Minute Sequence
**"From Idea to 262 Files While You Sleep: Building an AI Agent Swarm with Claude Code"**

---

## THE ONE FEELING
*(Rick Rubin)*

In the first two minutes, the audience should feel one thing: **awe at the impossible becoming inevitable.**

Not hype. Not "look how cool this tech is." Awe. The feeling you get watching a time-lapse of a cathedral being built, or hearing the first four notes of a symphony and knowing — without knowing how you know — that something enormous is about to happen.

Everything in this opening either serves that feeling or it gets cut.

---

## VISUAL PHILOSOPHY
*(Jony Ive)*

Nine images. Nine minds. The visual grammar is simple: **darkness, then light, then motion.**

We are not doing a slideshow. We are doing a slow reveal — the way you pull a white cloth off something that was always there, waiting to be seen. Each persona emerges from black. Not slides appearing. Not transitions fading. Characters arriving.

The visual rhythm is **slow, slower, then suddenly fast** — like a heartbeat accelerating. The audience should feel the momentum building before they consciously understand why.

No titles on screen until the very end. Let the faces speak first.

---

## SECOND-BY-SECOND TIMING

### 0:00 — 0:08 | THE VOID
**On screen:** Pure black. Nothing.

**Seth says:**
> "Last Tuesday night, I went to sleep with an idea."

*Pause. Two full seconds of silence.*

> "I woke up with a product."

*One more beat of silence. Let it land.*

**Visual:** Still black. The silence is load-bearing. Do not rush it.

**Music:** None yet. Silence is the first instrument.

---

### 0:08 — 0:18 | THE FIRST PRESENCE
**On screen:** Marcus Aurelius fades in from black — slow, 2-second dissolve. Full screen. No text. Marble robes, glass office. He looks like he's been waiting.

**Seth says:**
> "While I slept... nine minds went to work."

*Voice is quiet. Almost reverent.*

**Visual:** Marcus holds center frame for 3 full seconds. He is alone. He is in charge. The weight of Rome is in his posture.

**Music:** A single, low sustained tone begins — barely audible. Like the room itself started humming. Recommend: Hans Zimmer's approach — one note, a cello or pad, sitting under everything.

**Transition to next:** Not a cut. A slow 1.5-second cross-dissolve.

---

### 0:18 — 0:28 | THE VISIONARY
**On screen:** Steve Jobs. Black turtleneck, white room. Pure contrast against the dark universe we just left.

**Seth says:** *Nothing.* Silence for 4 seconds.

**Visual:** The white room is almost startling after the marble. That's intentional. Jony's note: the brightness should feel like an idea arriving.

**Music:** The tone holds. Maybe a second frequency joins — a minor third above. Still subtle.

**Transition:** Cross-dissolve, 1-second. Slightly faster than the first.

---

### 0:28 — 0:38 | THE BUILDER
**On screen:** Elon Musk. Flight suit, launchpad. The sky behind him is real — or feels real.

**Seth says:**
> "Someone to push past what's possible."

**Visual:** He should feel kinetic even in a still image. The launchpad gives the frame depth and scale. Hold 4 seconds.

**Transition:** 1-second cross-dissolve.

---

### 0:38 — 0:46 | THE FOUNDATION
**On screen:** Jensen Huang. Leather jacket, server room. Rows of lights behind him like a city at night.

**Seth says:**
> "Someone who understands what's underneath."

**Visual:** The server room should feel warm, not cold — those blinking lights are alive. Hold 3 seconds.

**Transition:** 0.8-second dissolve. We are accelerating.

---

### 0:46 — 0:54 | THE GUARDIAN
**On screen:** Margaret Hamilton. NASA. The code stack beside her. She looks like the most serious person in any room she's ever been in.

**Seth says:**
> "Someone who makes sure it doesn't fail."

**Visual:** The NASA setting gives the frame historical weight. This is what it looks like when software actually matters. Hold 3 seconds.

**Transition:** 0.8-second dissolve.

---

### 0:54 — 1:02 | THE SOUL
**On screen:** Rick Rubin. Zen studio. Beard, stillness, the sense that he has heard ten thousand songs and learned to love the one true note in each of them.

**Seth says:**
> "Someone to find the signal in the noise."

**Visual:** The zen studio should feel like breathing. White and warm. Hold 3 seconds.

**Transition:** 0.7-second dissolve. The sequence is now clearly accelerating.

---

### 1:02 — 1:10 | THE FORM
**On screen:** Jony Ive. White studio, wireframes. Everything in the frame is intentional — no accident in this room.

**Seth says:**
> "Someone to make it beautiful."

**Visual:** The wireframes in the background are quietly perfect. They suggest the skeleton of something not yet born. Hold 3 seconds.

**Transition:** 0.7-second dissolve.

---

### 1:10 — 1:17 | THE VOICE
**On screen:** Maya Angelou. Elegant. The fountain pen is a weapon and she knows it.

**Seth says:**
> "Someone to give it a voice."

**Visual:** She should feel timeless. The warmth in the image should contrast with the crisp geometry of Jony's frame before it. Hold 3 seconds.

**Transition:** 0.6-second dissolve. Almost a cut now.

---

### 1:17 — 1:24 | THE MARKET
**On screen:** Sara Blakely. Restaurant storefront. She looks like someone who has stood at a door like this and decided to walk through it anyway.

**Seth says:**
> "And someone to put it in front of the world."

**Visual:** The storefront is the most human setting in the sequence — real street, real light, real stakes. Hold 3 seconds.

**Transition:** Hard cut to black. Sudden. The audience should feel it.

---

### 1:24 — 1:38 | THE REVEAL
**On screen:** Black. Then a single line of white text fades in:

> **262 files.**
> **One night.**
> **Nine minds.**

Text holds for 6 seconds. No other elements. The number does the work.

**Seth says:** Nothing. Let the number breathe.

**Music:** The layered tones from earlier resolve — not loudly, but with a sense of arrival. The hum finds its chord.

---

### 1:38 — 2:00 | THE PIVOT
**On screen:** Text fades to black. Then a single question appears:

> **"How?"**

Hold 3 seconds. Then that text dissolves into your first workshop slide — whatever your opening content slide is. The transition should feel continuous, not like a mode-switch.

**Seth says:**
> "That's what the next 45 minutes is about. Not the theory of AI agents — you can read that anywhere. This is about what actually happened. What they argued about, what they got wrong, what they built that surprised even me."

*Pause.*

> "Let's start from the beginning."

**Music:** Fades to nothing as Seth begins talking. The silence signals: we are working now.

## MUSIC AND ATMOSPHERE
*(Rick Rubin)*

**What to avoid:** Anything that sounds like a tech conference. Upbeat corporate electronica. Anything with a drop. Anything that makes it feel like a product launch event you've seen before.

**What to find:** A single thread of sound that feels like the beginning of something large and quiet. Recommendations:

- **"Experience" by Ludovico Einaudi** — piano, restraint, space between the notes
- **"On the Nature of Daylight" by Max Richter** — strings, melancholy, time passing
- **Any Hans Zimmer single-tone build** — the *Interstellar* approach: one note that becomes everything

Start the music at 0:08 (Marcus arrives). Keep it under the voice, never competing. Fade at 1:38 when Seth begins his pivot monologue.

Volume curve: 0% at 0:00, ramp to 15% by 0:18, hold at 15-20% through the reveals, swell gently to 30% at 1:24 (the black moment), hold through the text reveal, then gracefully fade to 0% by 1:55.

## TECHNICAL NOTES — IMAGE OPTIMIZATION

*(Jony Ive)*

The images are large. Full-screen moments deserve full quality, but a presentation that lags is a presentation that loses the room. Optimize without compromising.

**Target specifications:**

| Use Case | Format | Max Width | Quality | Target File Size |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Keynote / PowerPoint | JPEG | 2560px | 85% | 400–600 KB each |
| Web / browser-based slides | WebP | 1920px | 80% | 150–250 KB each |
| Backup / print quality | PNG | 3840px | 100% | Source only |

**Conversion commands (ImageMagick):**
```bash
# Batch convert all persona images to optimized JPEG for Keynote
for f in *.png; do
  convert "$f" -resize 2560x1440^ -gravity center -extent 2560x1440 \
    -quality 85 -strip "${f%.png}-keynote.jpg"
done

# Batch convert to WebP for browser-based slides
for f in *.png; do
  cwebp -q 80 -resize 1920 1080 "$f" -o "${f%.png}-web.webp"
done
```

**Aspect ratio:** All images should be normalized to 16:9 (1920x1080 or 2560x1440). If any source images are a different ratio, crop to center — the faces should be the anchor point of each crop.

**Pre-load strategy:** If using a browser-based presentation (Reveal.js, Slides.com, etc.), pre-load all 9 images before the presentation begins. A single-image loading stutter during a cinematic sequence destroys the effect irreparably.

**Keynote / PowerPoint notes:**
- Set slide transitions to "Dissolve" — not "Fade Through Black" (which adds a harsh dark frame) but true cross-dissolve between images
- Duration: match the timings above (1.5s for first transition, stepping down to 0.6s by the end)
- Set each slide to advance automatically; do not rely on clicking during this sequence

## PRESENTER NOTES — WHAT MAKES THIS WORK

*(Rick Rubin)*

The script above is a skeleton. What makes it land is what Seth does in the silences.

**The pauses are not empty.** They are the most important moments. When Seth says "I woke up with a product" and then says nothing for two seconds — that silence is doing more work than any word could. Do not rush to fill it. The audience's imagination fills it better than any sentence.

**The voice should never perform.** This is not a TED talk with manufactured gravitas. Seth is telling the truth about something that actually happened to him. That's more interesting than any rehearsed enthusiasm. Quiet conviction is the register. Not whispering — present. Grounded.

**Stand still.** During the persona reveals (0:08–1:24), if Seth is visible on camera, he should be nearly motionless. The images are moving (conceptually). He is the still point.

**The pivot at 1:38 is where energy shifts.** The opening is cinematic and slow. The "That's what the next 45 minutes is about" line is where Seth becomes a teacher again — present, direct, ready to work. The transition in voice tone should be felt.

---

## FLOW INTO WORKSHOP CONTENT

The "How?" card at 1:38 is the bridge. Whatever Seth's first actual content slide is — the PRD, the agent architecture diagram, the file count breakdown — it should appear to answer "How?" directly.

Suggested first content slide after the opening:
- Title: **"It started with a one-page brief."**
- Or: **"Meet the system."** (showing the agent architecture at a glance)
- Or simply: the PRD itself, with Seth saying "This is what I gave them."

The opening has done its job if, when Seth says "Let's start from the beginning," the audience leans forward.
