# Great Minds Pipeline

**Ideas collide. You think.**

The Great Minds Pipeline is the orchestration engine behind a multi-agent AI agency that transforms product requirements into strategy documents, brand assets, marketing content, and full applications.

## What is Great Minds?

Great Minds is a design and development agency powered by 14 AI agent personas—including Steve Jobs, Elon Musk, Jensen Huang, and Margaret Hamilton—who process PRDs through a structured debate-plan-build-review-ship pipeline. The result isn't just output. It's intellectual companionship: brilliant minds wrestling with your questions.

## The 5 Pipeline Phases

### Phase 1: DEBATE (Rounds 1-2)
Steve Jobs and Elon Musk stake independent positions on all deliverable areas, then challenge each other through structured rounds. Steve pushes for beauty, simplicity, and emotional resonance. Elon pushes for feasibility, scalability, and speed to market. Phil Jackson logs the decisions. The goal: lock strategic decisions before building.

### Phase 2: PLAN (Round 3)
Directors define their teams by writing agent definitions. Steve hires designers, copywriters, and brand strategists. Elon hires market analysts, growth strategists, and team architects. Each agent gets a role definition with specific inputs, outputs, and a quality bar.

### Phase 3: BUILD (Rounds 4-8)
Sub-agents execute assignments in parallel. Each reads the PRD, the debate decisions, and their role definition. Output goes to `deliverables/{project}/drafts/`. Directors intervene if work drifts from the locked strategy.

### Phase 4: REVIEW (Round 9)
Steve reviews for taste, craft, and brand consistency. Elon reviews for feasibility, accuracy, and market alignment. Revisions go back to specific agents with targeted feedback.

### Phase 5: SHIP (Round 10)
Final deliverables are assembled. Both partners write a joint summary. Marcus Aurelius writes the retrospective. Learnings are saved to memory. The agency gets smarter with every project.

## What the Agency Produces

For every PRD processed:

- **Product Design Vision** — Steve leads, Elon challenges
- **Product/Market Fit Analysis** — Elon leads, Steve challenges
- **Customer Personas** — Who uses this and why
- **Team Personas** — Who builds this and how
- **Marketing Goals** — Measurable targets
- **Marketing Messaging** — Brand voice and copy
- **Joint Summary** — Synthesized consensus from both directors

## Core Principles

1. **No mediocrity.** If it's not worth building beautifully, it's not worth building.
2. **No hand-waving.** Every claim must survive first-principles scrutiny.
3. **No feature bloat.** Focus means saying no to good ideas so great ideas can breathe.
4. **No slow timelines.** Timelines are compressible. Default to faster.
5. **No vanity metrics.** Measure what matters for the business and the user.
