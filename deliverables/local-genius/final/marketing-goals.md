**153 is short of 300.** The gap is real. How we close it:

- **Repeat cycles**: The 90-day model above is a single pass. Direct outreach continues — by week 6, the GTM Lead has refined their pitch, conversion rates improve 20-30%. Adjusted direct outreach: ~110.
- **Compounding referrals**: Referral math above is conservative. If week-8 users are already referring by week 12, the curve steepens. Adjusted: ~20.
- **Local Facebook groups**: Austin has 15+ active local business Facebook groups (Austin Foodies, Austin Restaurant Workers, East Austin Business Network). GTM Lead posts case studies with permission. Estimate: 30 trials → 18 paid.
- **Google Business Profile optimization visible to other businesses**: When a restaurant's GBP jumps in ranking, their neighbors notice. Organic discovery effect. Estimate: 15 paid.

**Revised 90-day projection: 316 paying users (base case).** Stretch target of 500 requires the product-led loop to generate 40%+ above the base, which is possible but not guaranteed.

### 2.3 Week-by-Week Execution Plan

**WEEKS 1-2: Foundation**
- GTM Lead starts direct outreach — South Congress and East 6th Street restaurant districts. Target: 30 restaurants visited, 10 demos, 3-5 trials.
- Secure TRA Austin sponsorship for Month 2 event ($1,500).
- Brief 5 SCORE Austin mentors on LocalGenius (in-person meeting with mentor coordinator).
- Set up referral tracking infrastructure (unique referral links per user).
- Deploy "Posted by LocalGenius" watermark on all AI-generated social posts.
- **Milestone: First 5 paying customers.** These are the proof cases. Over-invest in their success.

**WEEKS 3-4: Learn and Iterate**
- Continue direct outreach — expand to N. Lamar, S. 1st, Rainey district. Target: 65 restaurants visited.
- Analyze first 5 customers: What content did they generate? Which features activated? What surprised them?
- First Weekly Digests go out. Monitor: open rate, time spent, share rate.
- Refine onboarding based on drop-off data. Where do people hesitate? What questions do they ask?
- Join 3 Austin local business Facebook groups. Observe, don't pitch yet.
- **Milestone: 20 paying customers. First data on 7-day activation rate.**

**WEEKS 5-6: First Event + Referral Launch**
- TRA Austin monthly event — GTM Lead demos to 30+ attendees. Target: 15 trials from event.
- Launch referral program in Weekly Digest: "Give a restaurant owner friend a free month."
- Publish first case study: "[Restaurant name] went from 3.8 to 4.3 stars in 30 days." Get owner permission, photograph them.
- Post case study in Austin Foodies Facebook group with owner's endorsement.
- Brief 5 more SCORE mentors.
- **Milestone: 60 paying customers. First referral conversions tracked.**

**WEEKS 7-8: Acceleration**
- Direct outreach now hitting 15 restaurants/day — GTM Lead has refined pitch to 90-second demo. Conversion rate improving.
- Second TRA event ($2,000 sponsorship). Target: 20 trials.
- First customers hitting 30-day mark — measure retention. THIS IS THE MOST IMPORTANT DATA POINT.
- If 30-day retention < 80%: STOP all growth activity. Diagnose and fix. Growth without retention is waste.
- If 30-day retention ≥ 80%: Increase referral incentive. Add "bring a friend" events.
- **Milestone: 130 paying customers. 30-day retention measured for first cohort.**

**WEEKS 9-10: Product-Led Loops Maturing**
- Referral program generating 5-8 new trials/week from existing users.
- "Posted by LocalGenius" watermark driving 40+ visits/week to landing page.
- Chamber of Commerce event — demo to mixed audience, target restaurant owners.
- Second case study with measurable ROI data: "LocalGenius generated 23 new customer calls in 30 days for [Restaurant]."
- Begin NPS survey for users active > 30 days.
- **Milestone: 200 paying customers. NPS data collected.**

**WEEKS 11-12: Push to Target**
- All channels running at steady state. Direct outreach, TRA, referrals, product-led all contributing.
- Third case study published.
- TRA newsletter feature goes live (negotiated as part of sponsorship package). Reaches 3,200 members.
- Compile retention report: cohort curves for Month 1, Month 2 users.
- **Milestone: 300+ paying customers. Retention validated. Decision point: scale or fix.**

**WEEK 13 (Transition): Growth Review**
- Publish internal growth report: which channels exceeded, which underperformed, what the retention data shows.
- If retention ≥ 80% and 300+ users: green light for vertical expansion planning (month 4).
- If retention < 80%: pause new acquisition, invest in product improvements. Re-measure at week 16.

## 3. Metrics Framework

### 3.1 Leading Indicators (Predict Retention)

These metrics are measurable within the first 7 days and predict whether a user will still be paying at day 30.

| Leading Indicator | Target | Why It Predicts Retention |
|-------------------|--------|--------------------------|
| **Features activated in first 48 hours** | ≥3 of 6 core features | Users who activate 3+ features in 48 hours retain at 2x the rate of those who activate 1-2 (B2SMB SaaS benchmark from Pendo 2024). |
| **First AI-generated content published** | Within 72 hours | The "first publish" is the commitment moment. Pre-publish users are 3x more likely to churn in week 2. |
| **Weekly Digest opened** | First digest opened within 24 hours | Digest engagement is the heartbeat. If they don't open the first one, they're not coming back. |
| **Conversational interactions in week 1** | ≥5 messages | Users who "talk" to LocalGenius in week 1 internalize the core metaphor. Under 5 interactions = they're treating it as a tool, not an employee. |
| **Review response accepted** | ≥1 in first 14 days | Accepting an AI-drafted review response builds trust. This is the "I trust this thing to speak for my business" threshold. |

### 3.2 Lagging Indicators (Measure Business Health)

| Lagging Indicator | Target (Month 3) | Target (Month 6) | Target (Month 12) |
|-------------------|:-----------------:|:-----------------:|:------------------:|
| 30-day retention | >80% | >82% | >85% |
| Monthly churn rate | <5% | <3.5% | <3% |
| MRR | $13,200 | $66,000 | $440,000 |
| NPS | Measured | >50 | >60 |
| CAC (blended) | <$150 | <$140 | <$120 |
| LTV/CAC ratio | >5x | >7x | >9x |
| Pro tier upgrade rate | 25% | 30% | 35% |
| Referral rate (% users who refer) | 5% | 8% | 12% |

### 3.3 Metrics We Do NOT Track in Months 1-3

- **Impressions / reach**: Vanity. Doesn't correlate with revenue.
- **Website traffic to LocalGenius marketing site**: Premature. We're doing direct outreach, not inbound.
- **Social media followers for LocalGenius brand**: We're building the clients' presence, not ours.
- **Feature usage frequency beyond activation**: Optimization signal, not survival signal. Track after product-market fit is confirmed.

## 4. Funnel-Stage Messaging Deployment

Per locked decision: Fear acquires → Curiosity considers → Relief converts → Pride retains.

### 4.1 AWARENESS — Competitive Fear (Top of Funnel)

**Channel**: Direct outreach (GTM Lead pitch), cold email, Facebook group posts, TRA event opener.

**Message architecture**: Specific, local, competitive. Name the gap.

| Format | Example Copy |
|--------|-------------|
| GTM Lead opening line (in-person) | "Hey — I was looking at your Google listing and you've got 12 reviews. The taco place two blocks over has 89. I can show you why in 60 seconds." |
| Cold email subject line | "28 reviews. Your neighbor has 147." |
| Facebook group post | "I analyzed 50 Austin restaurants' digital presence last week. 34 of them are invisible on Google compared to their nearest competitor. Here are the 3 things the visible ones have in common." (Genuine insight, not pitch. CTA: "DM me if you want your own analysis.") |
| TRA event opening slide | "The average Austin restaurant has a 3.9 Google rating and 47 reviews. The ones at 4.5+ with 200+ reviews see 23% more foot traffic. Here's the gap — and it's fixable." |

**Rules**: Always name specific, local competition or data. Never generic. Never say "AI." Never say "platform." Always offer a specific, tangible action within 5 minutes.

### 4.2 CONSIDERATION — Curiosity + Proof (Mid-Funnel)

**Channel**: Landing page, demo, TRA booth, case study.

**Message architecture**: Show, don't tell. Real business, real results, real owner.

| Format | Example Copy |
|--------|-------------|
| Landing page headline | "See what LocalGenius built for Rosa's Taqueria in 5 minutes." (Video demo with real owner reaction.) |
| Case study headline | "How Pecan Street Grill went from 3.8 to 4.4 stars and added 31 Google reviews in 28 days" |
| Demo CTA | "Tell me your restaurant name. I'll show you what your digital presence could look like — takes 3 minutes." |
| TRA booth interaction | Live generation: "What's your restaurant? Let me pull it up." → show their current Google listing gaps → generate improved version on the spot. |

**Rules**: Every claim must have a number. No aspirational language. Show the before/after. Let the owner see THEIR business, not a generic example.

### 4.3 CONVERSION — Relief + Delight (Sign-Up / Onboarding)

**Channel**: Onboarding flow, first-run experience, welcome sequence.

**Message architecture**: "Your business, handled." The weight lifts. Someone is finally in your corner.

| Format | Example Copy |
|--------|-------------|
| Onboarding welcome | "Tell me about your restaurant. Name, what you serve, where you are. I'll handle the rest." |
| First website generated | "Here's your new website. I built it from what you told me. Want to change anything? Just tell me." |
| First social post drafted | "I wrote your first Instagram post. Here's the preview. Tap to post — or tell me to try again." |
| First review response drafted | "You got a 4-star review from Sarah. Here's what I'd say back. Sound good?" |
| End of onboarding | "You're set. I'll handle your social posts, reviews, and SEO. Check in anytime — I'll send you a report every Monday." |

**Rules**: Warm, confident, simple. No settings screens. No tutorials. The product IS the onboarding.

### 4.4 RETENTION — Pride + Partnership (Ongoing)

**Channel**: Weekly Digest, in-app notifications, milestone celebrations.

**Message architecture**: "Look what WE accomplished." Owner feels proud, not dependent.

| Format | Example Copy |
|--------|-------------|
| Weekly Digest opening | "This week at [Restaurant Name]: 8 new Google reviews (4.7 avg), 340 website visits, 12 'call' taps. Your rating is now #4 for 'Mexican food in Austin.'" |
| Milestone notification | "You just hit 100 Google reviews. Only 12% of Austin restaurants have that. Your customers are noticing." |
| Monthly summary | "February recap: 23 social posts published, 31 reviews responded to, 1,200 website visits. Your Google ranking improved from #8 to #4 for 'tacos near me.'" |
| Renewal reinforcement (Month 2) | "In your first 30 days, LocalGenius generated an estimated 47 customer calls and 12 online reservations for [Restaurant]. Here's the breakdown." |

**Rules**: Always attribute results. Always use the owner's business name. Always compare to a relevant benchmark ("top 12% of Austin restaurants"). Never generic.

## 5. Product-Led Growth Mechanics

### 5.1 "Posted by LocalGenius" Watermark

Every AI-generated social post includes a subtle, non-intrusive watermark: a small "Posted by LocalGenius" text or icon in the corner.

- **Impression estimate**: 12 posts/user/month × 5 impressions/post × 300 users = 18,000 monthly impressions by month 3.
- **Expected traffic**: 0.5% CTR = 90 monthly visits.
- **Conversion**: 10% trial, 50% paid = 4-5 new users/month from this channel alone.
- **Owner opt-out**: Owners can remove the watermark on the Pro tier. This creates an upgrade incentive AND ensures the owners who keep it are genuine advocates.

### 5.2 Shareable Weekly Digest

The Weekly Digest includes a "Share this report" button that generates a public-facing version (no sensitive data — just highlights and metrics).

- Owner texts it to a fellow restaurant owner: "Look what this thing does for me."
- The shared version includes a CTA: "Want this for your restaurant? Start in 5 minutes."
- **Expected share rate**: 5% of digests shared (1 in 20 owners shares ~once/month).
- **Conversion from shares**: High intent — personal recommendation + visible results. 20% trial, 50% paid.

### 5.3 "Invite a Neighbor" Referral Program

- **Trigger**: After a user's first positive Weekly Digest (week 2+).
- **Incentive**: Referring owner gets one month free. Referred owner gets one month free. Simple.
- **Mechanic**: Unique referral link in the Weekly Digest and in the conversational interface ("Know another restaurant owner? Here's a link to give them a free month.").
- **Expected participation**: 8% of active users refer at least one business per quarter.
- **Cap**: Referral rewards cap at 3 months free per year per user (prevents gaming).

### 5.4 Case Study Auto-Generation

At month 2+, for users with strong results:
- LocalGenius auto-generates a case study draft from the owner's data (review improvements, traffic growth, content published).
- Presents to owner: "Your results are impressive. Want me to write this up as a success story you can share? I'll make you look great."
- If approved, published to LocalGenius case study library AND shared with GTM Lead for outreach collateral.
- Owner gets a professionally designed one-pager they can frame in their restaurant.

## 6. Vertical Expansion Gates

### 6.1 Gate Criteria (All Must Be Met)

| Gate | Metric | Threshold | Rationale |
|------|--------|-----------|-----------|
| **Retention proof** | 30-day retention for restaurant cohort | >80% for 2 consecutive months | Can't scale what doesn't retain. |
| **Active user base** | Active paying restaurant users | >300 | Enough data to understand churn patterns and attribution. |
| **CAC stability** | Blended organic CAC | <$150 for 2 consecutive months | Acquisition economics must be repeatable. |
| **NPS baseline** | NPS for users active >30 days | >40 (measured, trending toward 50) | Users must be genuinely satisfied, not just retained. |
| **AI quality validation** | Content approval rate (users approve AI output without edits) | >75% | AI must generate quality content for a new vertical without extensive retraining. |

### 6.2 Expansion Sequence

| Priority | Vertical | Why Next | Expansion Trigger Date (Estimated) |
|:--------:|----------|----------|:----------------------------------:|
| 1 | Hair salons / barbershops | Visual, social-heavy, review-driven. Similar content patterns to restaurants. Austin has ~1,800 addressable salons. | Month 4-5 |
| 2 | Dental / medical practices | High-value, review-sensitive, strong word-of-mouth. $79 tier natural fit. Austin has ~1,200 addressable practices. | Month 6-7 |
| 3 | Home services (plumbing, HVAC, electrical) | Lead attribution is critical — "Kevin the plumber" persona. Requires phone call tracking integration. Higher technical lift. | Month 8-9 |
| 4 | Auto repair / body shops | Review-driven, local-search dependent. Lower content frequency than restaurants. | Month 10-12 |

### 6.3 Per-Vertical Adaptation Cost

Each vertical expansion requires:
- **Prompt engineering**: 1-2 weeks. Vertical-specific content templates (menu descriptions ≠ haircut descriptions ≠ dental service descriptions).
- **Integration work**: 0-2 weeks. Some verticals need specific integrations (booking systems, insurance verification, service scheduling).
- **GTM adaptation**: 1 week. Identify vertical-specific associations, adjust outreach pitch, create first case study.
- **Total per-vertical cost**: 2-4 weeks of engineering + 1 week of GTM prep. No new hires required for first 2 expansions.
