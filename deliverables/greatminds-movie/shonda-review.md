# Shonda Rhimes -- Narrative Review

**Script:** Great Minds Agency -- The Movie (4:00)

## The Hook (Scene 1)
The opening question is strong -- "What happens when Steve Jobs and Elon Musk disagree about your product?" -- but you bury it under two seconds of silence and then a different line about how people use AI. Lead with the question IN the voiceover. Let the audience hear it, not just read it. The silence-then-pivot loses momentum exactly when you need to be grabbing throats.

## Tension & Pull-Through
Act 2 is solid architecture. The debate framework, the product cards, the board -- each scene raises a new question the next scene answers. But the transition from Act 2 to Act 3 (the board to "three failures") needs a harder pivot. You go from triumph to failure without a bridge. Add one line: "But before any of that worked..." -- give the audience permission to lean into the struggle.

## The Buffett Moment
"Warren Buffett called our plugin a hobby. He was right. We shipped it anyway." This is the single best line in the script. It is honest, funny, and defiant. But you use it as a punchline buried inside the board scene. Pull it forward. Tease it earlier -- maybe Scene 1 or 2 -- as a mystery. "One of our board members called the whole thing a hobby." Then pay it off in Scene 5 with the full reveal. That is how you make people stay.

## The Close
"We built a team that never sleeps. Now we are teaching it to dream." Beautiful. It lands. Do not touch it.

## What I Would Change to Make Someone Share This
1. Open the VO with the killer question, not the Google comparison.
2. Tease the Buffett line early -- create a narrative thread people want to see resolved.
3. Add a one-line bridge before the failures section to create dramatic contrast.
4. Scene 6 (three failures) is your most human moment -- let the VO breathe there. The vulnerability is what makes the triumph feel earned.

## Verdict
The bones are excellent. The story arc works: wonder, proof, struggle, transcendence. With three small adjustments this goes from "impressive demo" to "I need to send this to someone." Ship it.
