# LocalGenius Sites — 2-Minute Demo Script

**Runtime**: 2:00
**Format**: Narrator voiceover + screen walkthroughs
**Tone**: Human, warm, urgent — not a pitch deck

---

## OPENING: THE PROBLEM (0:00 - 0:25)

**NARRATOR:**
Maria runs a taco shop in Austin.

[SCREEN: Google search results showing "best tacos Austin" — Maria's shop is nowhere]

**NARRATOR:**
She's been there eleven years. Her carnitas are legendary. People drive forty minutes just to stand in line.

[SCREEN: Yelp page showing 4.8 stars, 847 reviews, "This place changed my life" testimonials]

**NARRATOR:**
And when someone searches for her? Nothing. Not on the first page. Not on the second. Because Maria doesn't have a website. She has a Facebook page she made in 2016 and a phone number written on a napkin behind the register.

[SCREEN: Facebook business page — outdated hours, blurry photo, last post: "Happy Thanksgiving 2023"]

**NARRATOR:**
She's not lazy. She's not behind. She's *busy*. She gets up at four AM to marinate the pork. She doesn't have time to learn Squarespace. She doesn't have three hundred dollars for some guy on Fiverr.

[SCREEN: Beat. The Facebook page lingers. Then slowly fades to white]

**NARRATOR:**
What Maria needs... is a little bit of magic.

---

## THE PRODUCT (0:25 - 1:15)

[SCREEN: Clean interface. Simple chat window. Text appears: "Hi! I'm here to help you get online. What's your business called?"]

**NARRATOR:**
This is LocalGenius. You tell it your name.

[SCREEN: User types "Maria's Taco Shop"]

**NARRATOR:**
You upload a photo. Any photo. The one on your phone from yesterday is fine.

[SCREEN: Photo picker opens. User selects image of food on a counter. Simple. Real.]

**NARRATOR:**
You tell it what you do — in one sentence.

[SCREEN: User types "Family-owned taco shop serving Austin since 2013"]

**NARRATOR:**
And then... you wait five seconds.

[SCREEN: Build animation begins — subtle, elegant progress states:
"Building something beautiful..."
"Pulling your reviews..."
"Optimizing your photos..."
"Almost there..."]

**NARRATOR:**
Not five hours. Not five days.
Five. Seconds.

[SCREEN: The build animation completes. A soft transition...]

---

## THE REVEAL (1:15 - 1:40)

[SCREEN: Device frame appears — an iPhone mockup. Inside it: a gorgeous, professional restaurant website. Hero image. Business name. Hours. A "Call Now" button. Below the device: "Your site is live."]

**NARRATOR:**
And there it is.

[SCREEN: Hold on the reveal. Let it breathe. URL bar shows: mariastacoshop.localgenius.site]

**NARRATOR:**
Her name. Her story. Her reviews from Google — pulled in automatically. Hours, phone number, address. All real. All hers.

[SCREEN: Slowly scroll the site inside the device frame — menu section with prices, photo gallery, "Made with LocalGenius" in the footer]

**NARRATOR:**
Maria didn't design this. She didn't pick a template. She didn't choose a font. She just... told us who she was.

[SCREEN: The URL bar. Clean. Real. mariastacoshop.localgenius.site]

**NARRATOR:**
And now she exists.

---

## THE WOW MOMENT (1:40 - 2:00)

[SCREEN: Back to chat interface. New message from Maria: "Hey, I want to add my new breakfast tacos to the menu"]

**NARRATOR:**
And here's the thing that changes everything.

[SCREEN: Response appears: "Done! I added 'Breakfast Tacos' to your menu with a $6.99 price. Here's what it looks like now." Below: a preview of the updated site.]

**NARRATOR:**
She doesn't have to call anyone. She doesn't have to log into anything. She just... talks. And her website updates itself.

[SCREEN: Site preview showing the new menu item]

**NARRATOR:**
This isn't a website builder.

[SCREEN: Cut to black. White text fades in: "LocalGenius"]

**NARRATOR:**
It's the feeling of legitimacy. For the first time.

[SCREEN: Tagline appears below: "Your business deserves to exist online."]

**NARRATOR:**
And Maria? She's already back in the kitchen.

[SCREEN: Final beat. Logo. End.]

---

## DIRECTOR'S NOTES

- **Pacing**: The build sequence (0:25-1:15) should feel unhurried. Let the simplicity land.
- **The Reveal**: This is the whole product. Hold on it. The gasp happens here.
- **Narrator tone**: Warm, matter-of-fact. Think documentary narrator, not ad voice.
- **Music**: Minimal. Piano underneath. Swell slightly at reveal, then back down.
- **No confetti**: The restraint *is* the design.

---

*"The reveal moment — the user seeing their live site — is the entire product."*
