# Steve Jobs — Round 1 Positions
## LocalGenius Sites

---

### Product Naming

**Call it "Presence."**

Not "LocalGenius Sites." That's a feature name. It describes what you built, not what you give people. Nobody falls in love with a feature list.

Presence. One word. It's what these businesses don't have online. It's what we give them in five minutes. "Your Presence is live." That's the notification. That's the emotion.

LocalGenius is the AI assistant. Presence is what the AI creates for you. The relationship is clear: "LocalGenius built my Presence." Two brands, one story.

---

### Design Philosophy

**This must feel like magic, not software.**

The user answers seven questions. Then something impossible happens: a real, beautiful, living website appears with *their* business on it. Not a template with blanks filled in. A thing that looks like someone who actually gives a damn created it.

The design philosophy is invisible craft. Every pixel justified. No lorem ipsum anywhere. No stock photos of handshakes. The template selection happens silently — the user never chooses between "Template A" and "Template B" because that's our job, not theirs.

Two templates at launch. Not five. Not ten. Two that are *genuinely beautiful*. I'd rather ship one masterpiece than a gallery of mediocrity.

---

### User Experience: The First 30 Seconds

**The reveal is the product.**

Question seven answered. Three-second pause — not instant, that feels cheap. The AI is "building" (we show just enough progress to create tension). Then: their business appears. Real domain. Real content. Real business hours.

The gasp is mandatory. If they don't gasp, we failed.

After the reveal: one button. "This looks right" or "Something's wrong." Binary. No settings, no customization flow, no "would you like to change the font?" Approval is the only friction. Everything else was our job.

---

### Brand Voice

**Confident. Warm. Never clever.**

"Your business, live in five minutes" — that's the voice. Direct promise. No asterisks.

The AI speaks like a competent assistant, not a chatbot. "I updated your hours" not "I've successfully processed your hours modification request!" No exclamation points. No emoji. No "Woohoo, you did it!"

We are not their friend. We are their unfair advantage. The voice should feel like hiring someone who actually knows what they're doing.

---

### What to Say NO To

**No customization in v1. Zero.**

The moment we add "choose your color" we've lost. We've told them: "You need to have opinions about design." They don't. They have a business to run.

Say no to:
- Theme pickers
- Font dropdowns
- "Advanced settings"
- CMS access "for power users"
- Analytics dashboards (in v1)
- Template browsing
- Multiple page types

We ship the decision. They ship their business. That's the deal.

---

### The Emotional Hook

**They feel *seen* for the first time.**

Here's what happens to a plumber who's been running off Google Maps and Facebook for eight years: He tells an AI about his business for two minutes. Then he sees a real website with his name, his services, his hours — live on the internet, looking like it cost $5,000.

And nobody asked him to drag and drop anything. Nobody asked him to pick between Raleway and Montserrat. Nobody made him feel stupid.

The emotion isn't "this is convenient." The emotion is: *"Finally, someone made this for me."*

That's why they'll pay $79/month. Not for features. For the feeling that their business matters enough for a real website. We're not selling websites. We're selling legitimacy.
