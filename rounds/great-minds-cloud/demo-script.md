# Shipyard Demo Script
### 2 Minutes | One Take | The Moment Everything Changes

---

**[SCREEN: Black. A cursor blinks.]**

NARRATOR:
You know that idea you've had for three years?

The one you explain at dinner parties. The one that makes people say "you should really build that."

**[SCREEN: A notes app opens. We see fragments: "fitness app for..." "marketplace that connects..." "better way to..."]**

NARRATOR:
The problem isn't that you don't have the idea. The problem is that between you and that idea is a wall. Designers. Developers. Strategy. Architecture. Pricing models. User flows.

You're one person. You need a team. And a team costs six figures and six months you don't have.

**[SCREEN: Hard cut to Shipyard landing page. Clean. A single input field: "Paste your idea."]**

NARRATOR:
This is Shipyard. And I'm about to show you what happens when you paste an idea.

**[SCREEN: User types into the field — a few sentences about a "neighborhood tool lending app." We see them select "$500 - Starter." They click "Launch Project."]**

NARRATOR:
Watch the screen.

**[SCREEN: The page transitions. A debate interface appears. Empty for a beat. Then—]**

**[SCREEN: An agent avatar appears: "Steve Jobs — Product Visionary." A message populates: "The tool lending is a feature, not a product. What's the real transformation you're offering?"]**

NARRATOR:
Steve Jobs just asked you a question.

**[SCREEN: Another avatar: "Elon Musk — Systems Architect." His message: "Trust infrastructure first. Without verified identity and damage accountability, this fails at scale."]**

NARRATOR:
And Elon Musk just disagreed with him.

**[SCREEN: The debate continues. Messages stack. We see "Designer," "Growth Strategist," "Market Analyst" — each pushing, questioning, building. A progress bar shows: "Debate Phase: 23% Complete."]**

NARRATOR:
Fourteen agents. Fourteen perspectives. They're not performing consensus. They're performing *friction* — the kind of friction that sharpens an idea into something real.

**[SCREEN: The debate intensifies. Quick cuts show: "The onboarding is wrong—" "What about deposit handling?" "Push notifications are table stakes, not a feature—"]**

NARRATOR:
This isn't a chatbot telling you your idea is great. This is a room full of brilliant minds who won't let you ship something half-baked.

**[SCREEN: Progress bar jumps: "Building Phase: 67% Complete." The debate panel minimizes. A file list begins populating: "brand-strategy.md" ... "user-flows.png" ... "landing-page/" ...]**

NARRATOR:
And when they're done arguing? They build.

**[SCREEN: Completion animation. A download button appears with a clean ZIP icon. We see a preview of the deliverables: a styled landing page, a PRD document, brand guidelines, a database schema diagram.]**

NARRATOR:
Forty-five minutes ago, this was three sentences in a notes app. Now it's a brand strategy, user flows, a working landing page, and the architecture to actually build it.

**[SCREEN: User clicks download. The ZIP opens on their desktop. Real files. Real code. Real starting point.]**

**[SCREEN: Cut back to the Shipyard homepage. The input field blinks, waiting.]**

NARRATOR:
That idea you've had for three years?

**[SCREEN: Hold on the input field.]**

NARRATOR:
Paste it.

**[SCREEN: Shipyard logo fades in. Tagline: "Where ideas become real."]**

---

*End of demo. Total runtime: 1:58*
