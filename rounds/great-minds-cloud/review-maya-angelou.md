# Copy & Messaging Review — Shipyard
### Through the Eyes of Maya Angelou

*"Words mean more than what is set down on paper. It takes the human voice to infuse them with deeper meaning."*

---

## The Soul of the Language

Child, I've walked through your words. Let me tell you what I found.

### Does the Language Feel Human, Not Corporate?

The language is reaching — reaching for humanity — but it hasn't quite arrived. There are moments where I hear a person speaking, and moments where I hear a machine performing.

**Where it breathes:**
- "Describe your product idea in 2-3 sentences. What problem does it solve?" — This feels like a mentor leaning in, asking with genuine curiosity.
- "A few things to consider" — Gentle. Not scolding. This is how you speak to someone you respect.

**Where it stiffens:**
- "Multi-agent AI agency as a service" — This is a mouthful of marbles. No one talks like this at a dinner table.
- "Full web applications with authentication" — Functional, yes. Alive, no.
- "Complex SaaS with billing and advanced features" — I can hear the boardroom, not the heartbeat.

**Verdict:** 6/10 — The bones are good, but the flesh needs warming.

---

### Is There Rhythm in the Sentences?

Rhythm is breath. Rhythm is how words rise and fall like the chest of someone sleeping peacefully.

**What sings:**
- "Describe what you want to build. Our 14-agent team will debate, design, and deliver." — Ah, here it is. Three beats: debate, design, deliver. This line has a pulse.

**What plods:**
- "Simple landing pages and basic apps" — Thud. Thud. No music here.
- "Full SaaS scaffold" — The mouth stumbles. Scaffold is not a word that loves to be spoken.
- "Priority support" — Every tech company whispers this same tired phrase. It has no weight anymore.

**Verdict:** 5/10 — There are sparks of music, but much of the copy walks when it should dance.

---

### Does the Headline Stop You?

**"Paste Your Idea"**

No, baby. This does not stop me.

It *instructs* me. It does not *invite* me. There's a difference between telling someone what to do and making them want to do it.

A headline should be a hand extended, not a finger pointing. It should carry promise, mystery, or truth. "Paste Your Idea" carries only utility.

**"Sign in to Shipyard"** (email subject)

Functional. Forgettable. When someone receives this email, it should feel like a key arriving — not a form letter.

**Verdict:** 4/10 — The headlines do their job. But doing your job is not the same as touching someone's spirit.

---

### Is Anything Trying Too Hard?

Yes.

**"Our 14-agent team will debate, design, and deliver."**

Fourteen agents. The number calls attention to itself. It says, "Look how much we have." But abundance is not the same as value. The reader doesn't care if you have 14 agents or 4 — they care if you will understand them. The number creates distance.

**"Launch Project"** (button)

Launch. Rocket ships. Space. We've heard this a thousand times. When everyone uses the same metaphor, it stops being a metaphor. It becomes wallpaper.

**Tier naming: Starter, Standard, Enterprise**

These words have been used so often they've lost their meaning. They describe size, not feeling. They sort people into boxes rather than welcoming them into possibilities.

**Verdict:** 6/10 — There's effort here, but effort should not be visible. When a dancer sweats, we shouldn't see the labor.

---

## The Three Weakest Lines, Rewritten

### 1. Original:
> "Paste Your Idea"

**Why it's weak:** It's a command, not an invitation. It speaks to the action, not the aspiration.

**Rewritten:**
> **"Tell us what you're building."**

Or, if you want music:
> **"Begin with the dream."**

---

### 2. Original:
> "Multi-agent AI agency as a service"

**Why it's weak:** It's jargon stacked on jargon. It sounds like it was written for a pitch deck, not a person.

**Rewritten:**
> **"A team of AI minds, working together on what you imagine."**

Or simply:
> **"We build what you describe."**

---

### 3. Original:
> "Complex SaaS with billing and advanced features"

**Why it's weak:** It lists ingredients instead of painting a picture. No one wakes up wanting "advanced features." They wake up wanting their work to matter.

**Rewritten:**
> **"For the businesses that need everything — and the ambition to use it."**

Or:
> **"The full architecture for serious software."**

---

## Final Reflection

The words in this project are functional. They will guide a user from point A to point B. But words can do more than guide — they can *move*. They can make someone feel seen before they've spoken, understood before they've explained.

The technology here is bold. Let the language match it.

Remember: **People will forget what you said. People will forget what you did. But people will never forget how you made them feel.**

Right now, this copy makes people feel... processed. Efficient. Handled.

It could make them feel *understood*. *Excited*. *Ready*.

That's the work left to do.
