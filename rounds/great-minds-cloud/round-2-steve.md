# Steve Jobs — Round 2 Response

---

## Where Elon Gets It Wrong

### The "Productized Service First" Trap

Elon wants us to start with $2,500 done-for-you projects. He's optimizing for *revenue validation* when he should be optimizing for *product discovery*.

Here's the problem: when you charge $2,500 and deliver manually, you learn what high-touch customers want. You don't learn what self-serve customers need. Different populations. Different expectations. Different friction points.

Worse — you build a services company culture. Your team learns to customize, to accommodate, to say yes. That's the opposite muscle from building a product that says "this is how it works."

We're not building a consulting shop. We're building a product.

### "Nobody needs to watch agents think"

This is Elon's biggest miss. He wants a progress bar. I want to show the debate.

He's optimizing for *information efficiency*. I'm optimizing for *emotional engagement*.

When you see Jensen challenge your architecture and Oprah ask who this is really for — that's not wasted UI. That's the moment the user realizes this isn't ChatGPT with a different skin. That friction, that back-and-forth, is the soul of the product.

A progress bar says "wait." A visible debate says "watch this."

---

## Defending My Positions

### Why Design Quality Matters Here

Elon will say: ship ugly, iterate fast. But this isn't a B2B tool where users tolerate pain for ROI. Our users are non-technical founders. They judge quality by feel. By craft. By whether the experience respects their time.

If the landing page feels like every other AI tool, they'll assume the product is like every other AI tool. First impression is the only impression.

The design IS the differentiation. When everything uses Claude under the hood, the experience layer is your moat.

---

## Where Elon Is Right

I'll concede three things:

1. **The pricing model needs work.** $299/month with credits is confusing. Per-project pricing is cleaner. He's right that subscription without clear usage creates resentment.

2. **The API rate limits are real.** At scale, Claude's limits will hurt. This is a physics problem, not an architecture problem. We need to plan for it.

3. **Real-time WebSocket dashboards can wait.** Showing the debate doesn't require engineering complexity. A polling mechanism that updates every few seconds delivers 90% of the magic at 10% of the cost.

---

## My Non-Negotiables (Locked)

### 1. The Name is Shipyard

Not "Great Minds Cloud." One word. Memorable. Evocative. This is non-negotiable.

### 2. Visible Agent Debate in v1

Users must see agents arguing, not just a progress bar. This is the differentiator. We can simplify the implementation, but the concept ships in v1.

### 3. No Signup Wall for First Experience

Paste your idea, see agents activate. Prove value before asking for anything. Friction is the enemy.

---

## The Real Disagreement

Elon wants to validate revenue first. I want to validate magic first.

He's building a funnel. I'm building a feeling.

Both matter. But if we ship something that works but doesn't *wow*, we've lost. There are a hundred AI coding tools. There's only one chance to be the one people remember.
