# Round 2: Elon Musk — Chief Product & Growth Officer

## Where Steve's Beauty Gets in the Way of Shipping

**1. "Sous" is a branding exercise when we need traction.**

You want to rename the product before we have 100 customers? That's backwards. LocalGenius is fine. It's searchable. It's descriptive. "Sous" sounds like a VC pitch, not a product restaurant owners Google for. Name changes are what you do at scale, not when you're trying to find product-market fit.

**First ship, then brand.** You can't polish a product that doesn't exist yet.

**2. The "Holy Shit 60-second video" is a 2-week production nightmare.**

"A 60-second video with blurred dashboard, specific metrics, captions..." — Who's shooting this? Who's editing? What restaurant is giving us permission? This isn't opening a Keynote and dragging in assets. This is hiring a videographer, scheduling shoots, getting legal releases.

**Meanwhile, a Loom video from our founder explaining the product ships TODAY.** It won't be beautiful. It will convert. Perfection is the enemy of momentum.

**3. "No free trial without credit card" murders your top-of-funnel.**

You just cut your trial signups by 60-80%. SMB SaaS data is clear: requiring credit cards up-front converts better *if* you have strong brand trust. We don't. We're unknown. Asking for $99 up-front before they've seen the product work? You're optimizing for revenue per signup at the cost of total signups.

**Better:** Freemium tier (1 location, limited posts). No credit card. When they see results, they upgrade. Dropbox didn't ask for credit cards. Neither should we.

## Where I Was Wrong (and Steve Is Right)

**1. "LocalGenius" actually does suck.**

Fine. It does sound like 2007 SEO garbage. But we're not rebranding to "Sous" in week 1. **Compromise:** We ship as LocalGenius, but the domain is getous.com or trysous.com (if available), and the product internally is "Sous." Rebrand the marketing site in 30 days once we have proof of concept.

**2. Brand voice matters more than I gave credit for.**

"Your marketing, handled" is *significantly* better than "AI-powered marketing automation." Steve's right — restaurant owners don't care about our tech stack. They care about relief. His emotional hook (the 11 PM guilt) is sharp. I'd ship that copy tomorrow.

**Where Steve wins:** Positioning isn't scope creep. It's conversion optimization. His copy is tighter, clearer, and more emotionally resonant. Keep it.

**3. One price, not three tiers.**

I said cut the tiers. Steve said cut the tiers. We agree. $99/month. One product. Decision paralysis is real. Simplicity wins. Done.

## Where Technical Simplicity Still Wins

**Steve's "invisible intelligence" vision requires infrastructure we don't have yet.**

"The dashboard should show what's already done, not what you need to do." Beautiful idea. Requires:
- Cron jobs auto-generating posts
- Auto-publishing to Instagram/Facebook via API
- Review monitoring + auto-responses
- Revenue attribution from reservations

That's not a 3-day build. That's the *core product*. The pricing page doesn't need to wait for that. **Ship the page now. Build the magic later.**

**Static HTML + Loom videos + PostHog = ships this week.**
Steve's 60-second produced video = ships in 3 weeks (optimistically).

I'll take 70% conversion on an imperfect page today over 100% conversion on a perfect page in 3 weeks. Compounding growth starts when you ship, not when you're "ready."

## My Top 3 Non-Negotiables

### 1. **Ship the pricing page in 3 days, not 3 weeks.**
- Static HTML/CSS. No framework overhead.
- Loom testimonials (imperfect but real). We'll upgrade video quality later.
- PostHog for 5 core events. No A/B testing infrastructure week 1.

**Why this matters:** Every week we delay is a week of zero compounding growth. Speed is a feature.

### 2. **Freemium tier + paid tier. No "trial with credit card" gate.**
- **Free tier:** 1 location, 10 AI posts/month, email support only.
- **Pro tier:** $99/month, unlimited locations/posts, priority support, advanced analytics.
- Freemium converts 2-5%, but gets 10x more signups. That's math, not opinion.

**Why this matters:** We don't have brand trust yet. Lower the activation barrier. Get users in. Show results. Then monetize.

### 3. **Distribution strategy ships alongside pricing page.**
- Referral program: "Invite 3 restaurant owners, get $50 credit."
- Public case studies with attribution (Mario's Pizzeria + link to their Yelp).
- SEO content: 10 blog posts targeting "how to get more Google reviews."

**Why this matters:** A beautiful pricing page with zero traffic is a $0 business. Growth loops > perfect design.

---

**Bottom line:** Steve's positioning and emotional hooks are sharp. Keep them. But we're not delaying launch for produced video or a rebrand. Ship fast, iterate faster. The product that ships in 3 days with 80% quality beats the product that ships in 3 weeks at 100% quality.

**Let's compromise:**
- Use Steve's copy and brand voice.
- Ship with Loom videos and static HTML this week.
- Upgrade to produced video in 30 days once we have revenue and proof.
- Soft-launch as "Sous by LocalGenius" to test the name without full commitment.

The best argument against perfection is momentum. Let's go.
