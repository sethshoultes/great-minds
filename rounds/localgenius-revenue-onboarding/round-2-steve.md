# Round 2: Steve Jobs — Chief Design & Brand Officer

## Challenging Elon's Weakest Positions

### 1. Freemium Is a Race to the Bottom
Elon wants a free tier. "1 location, 10 posts/month." This is deeply wrong.

**The flaw:** You're training users to expect free. Restaurant owners who sign up for free aren't customers — they're tourists. They'll use your free tier until something shinier comes along, and they'll never convert because you've anchored them to $0.

Stripe learned this. Dropbox learned this. Free tiers attract the wrong users. The users who *need* this product will pay $99. The ones who won't pay weren't going to succeed anyway.

**Counter-proposal:** 14-day trial, credit card required. Friction filters for intent. We want 25 paying customers, not 1,000 freeloaders.

### 2. Referral Programs Are Growth Theater
"Every user gets 3 invite codes. Referrer gets $25 credit."

**The problem:** You're incentivizing people to spam their network before they've even experienced value. Dropbox's referral program worked because the product was *so good* people wanted their friends to have it. You're putting the cart before the horse.

Show me one SaaS company that hit $100K MRR through referral codes in their first 90 days. You can't, because it doesn't happen. Referrals are a growth *multiplier*, not a growth *engine*. First, build something worth referring.

### 3. "Ship in 3 Days" Is Engineer Cosplay
Elon says: "Static pricing page + Loom videos. Ship in 3 days."

Sure. And it'll look like a landing page from 2012. No soul. No magic. Just another SaaS graveyard headstone.

**The truth Elon won't say:** Fast is worthless if it's forgettable. I'd rather ship in 10 days with a page that makes someone stop scrolling than ship in 3 days with HTML tables and Times New Roman.

Speed is important. But speed without craft is just noise.

## Defending My Positions

### Why "Sous" Matters (And "LocalGenius" Doesn't)
Elon will say: "Names don't matter. Google was a typo. Execution matters."

**Wrong.** Google *sounded* like the future. LocalGenius sounds like a WordPress plugin from 2009.

A great name is a shortcut to brand equity. "Sous" does three things instantly:
1. **Memorable** — One syllable. Easy to spell. Easy to say.
2. **Metaphor** — Restaurant owners *get it*. A sous chef is your right hand.
3. **Premium** — It signals sophistication, not desperation.

When you tell another restaurant owner "I use Sous for my marketing," you sound smart. When you say "I use LocalGenius," you sound like you fell for a Facebook ad.

### Why Zero Interface Is the Right Interface
Elon wants dashboards with buttons. "Create Post." "Schedule Review Response."

**This is the opposite of what busy restaurant owners need.**

They don't want another tool to learn. They want to log in, see that their marketing is *already handled*, and get back to work. The dashboard should be a report card, not a control panel.

Apple doesn't make you configure your iPhone's antenna settings. It just works. Same principle here.

### Why One Price Is Right
Elon might argue: "Three tiers gives users choice. Basic/Pro/Enterprise is standard."

**Standard is the problem.** Three tiers create decision paralysis. "Am I a Basic user or a Pro user? What if I need Enterprise later?"

One price says: "This is what it costs. You're either in or you're out." Confidence. Clarity. No games.

## Where Elon Is Right (Intellectual Honesty)

### 1. Distribution Is the Actual Problem
Elon nailed it: "This PRD has ZERO distribution strategy."

He's right. We can build the most beautiful page in the world, but if no one sees it, we've built a monument in the desert.

**I concede:** We need a distribution plan. Not his referral theater, but something real:
- **Partnerships with POS systems** (Toast, Square) — be in the ecosystem.
- **Local restaurant associations** — speak at conferences, sponsor events.
- **Case studies with attribution** — Elon's right here. Mario shares his results, his network sees it.

### 2. Page Load Speed Matters More Than I Admitted
"<1s load time, not <3s." Elon's correct.

Every second is a chance for someone to bounce. If we're asking restaurant owners to trust us with their marketing, the page better load instantly. No excuses.

**I concede:** <1s load time is the standard. Use a CDN. Compress everything. No compromises.

### 3. ROI Calculator Is Useless
Elon's right to cut it. No one trusts our math. They trust *other restaurant owners* saying "this worked."

Show the before/after. Show the Yelp screenshot. Show the revenue graph. That's the ROI calculator.

## My Top 3 Non-Negotiables

### 1. The Name Is "Sous" — Not Negotiable
If we ship as "LocalGenius," we've already lost. This is the hill I die on.

The brand is the product. The product is the brand. Get the name wrong, and everything downstream suffers.

### 2. One Price: $99/Month — Not Negotiable
No tiers. No freemium. No "contact sales."

Simplicity is our competitive advantage. The moment we add pricing complexity, we become every other SaaS company. We're better than that.

### 3. Zero-Interface Dashboard Philosophy — Not Negotiable
The dashboard shows what's *already done*, not what you need to do.

This is the design principle that separates us from every other marketing tool. If we ship a button-heavy dashboard, we're just another task manager. We're supposed to *eliminate* tasks, not create them.

---

**Final word:** Elon optimizes for speed. I optimize for memorability. We need both. Ship fast, but ship something worth remembering. Otherwise, we're just adding to the noise.
