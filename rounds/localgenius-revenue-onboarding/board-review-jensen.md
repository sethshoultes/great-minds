# Board Review: LocalGenius Revenue & Onboarding
**Reviewer**: Jensen Huang, NVIDIA CEO
**Date**: 2026-04-16
**Project**: localgenius-revenue-onboarding

---

## The Moat Question: What Compounds?

**VERDICT: NO MOAT. PURE SERVICE PLAY.**

- Pricing page doesn't compound
- Testimonials don't compound
- Copy doesn't compound
- Zero network effects
- Zero data flywheel
- Every competitor can copy this in 48 hours

**What SHOULD compound:**
- Every review response trains the model on restaurant-specific voice
- Every post generates engagement data → better content predictions
- Multi-restaurant data → cross-learning (Mexican restaurants learn from Italian)
- Seasonal patterns → predictive posting calendars
- Review sentiment → menu optimization insights

**You're building lipstick. Not building the AI that gets smarter.**

---

## AI Leverage: Where's the 10x?

**VERDICT: AI IS INVISIBLE. THAT'S THE PROBLEM.**

Current PRD mentions AI once. In a quote. From me.

**Where AI should 10x outcomes:**
- Review responses in <60 seconds (not next day)
- Generate 30 days of posts from single menu photo
- Real-time "post this now" recommendations (lunch rush started, weather changed)
- Predictive review alerts ("Sarah's table had 20min wait, send recovery offer")
- Voice cloning from 3 owner samples → authentic responses at scale

**You're using AI to replace a VA. Use it to replace impossible.**

---

## Unfair Advantage We're Not Building

**VERDICT: PLAYING FAIR IN COMMODITY MARKET.**

**Missed advantages:**
1. **Aggregated review intelligence** — 660K restaurants = massive training corpus
   - "Restaurants like yours respond to 'slow service' complaints this way..."
   - Competitors have 1 restaurant's data. You should have 10,000.

2. **Platform lock-in through learned voice** — 90 days in, AI knows owner's tone perfectly
   - Switching cost = rebuilding voice model from scratch
   - Currently switching cost = $0

3. **Predictive revenue attribution** — "This Instagram post drove 12 reservations"
   - Google/Facebook won't give you this. You can infer it.
   - Becomes ROI proof → higher retention → higher LTV

4. **Multi-location intelligence** — Chain restaurant playbook
   - "Denver location's Taco Tuesday posts outperform. Roll to Phoenix?"
   - Franchises will pay 10x for this

**You're building features. Competitors have features. Build the unfair thing.**

---

## Platform vs Product: What's Missing

**VERDICT: THIS IS A WORDPRESS PLUGIN, NOT A PLATFORM.**

**To become platform:**

1. **Third-party integrations marketplace**
   - POS systems (Toast, Square, Clover) → auto-post new menu items
   - Reservation systems (OpenTable, Resy) → "We're fully booked" social posts
   - Delivery platforms (DoorDash, Uber Eats) → review aggregation
   - Rev share: 20% of integration upsells

2. **LocalGenius API** — Let others build on your review/post intelligence
   - Restaurant consultants pay for batch voice analysis
   - Marketing agencies white-label the posting engine
   - Franchise HQ monitors 47 locations via API

3. **Data products** — Sell insights, not just automation
   - "Q1 Restaurant Marketing Benchmark Report" ($299)
   - "Your competition posted 4.2x more than you" alerts
   - Industry intelligence becomes separate revenue stream

4. **Template marketplace** — Owners sell high-performing post templates
   - Top post from Italian restaurants: $29 one-time purchase
   - Creator economy inside restaurant marketing
   - Platform takes 30% cut

**Products serve customers. Platforms create ecosystems.**
