# Steve Jobs — Chief Design & Brand Officer
## LocalGenius Revenue & Onboarding: Design & Brand Positions

### Product Naming: KILL "LocalGenius"
It's trying too hard. It sounds like a yellow pages SEO scam from 2007.

**New name: Sous**

One syllable. A sous chef is the right-hand who handles everything while the chef creates. That's what this product is — the AI sous chef that handles your marketing while you cook. Memorable. Defensible. It says everything without explaining anything.

### Design Philosophy: Invisible Intelligence
The product should feel like magic, not machinery. Restaurant owners don't want to "use marketing software" — they want their marketing to *just happen*.

**Core principle:** Zero interface is the best interface.

The dashboard should show you what's already done, not what you need to do. No buttons labeled "Create Social Post" — just "Your posts this week" with gorgeous previews already published. The AI works in the background like a great employee. You check in, you nod, you get back to the kitchen.

Apple doesn't sell you a computer — they sell you what you'll create with it. We're not selling "AI marketing automation" — we're selling full restaurants and five-star reviews.

### First 30 Seconds: The "Holy Shit" Moment
You land on the page. A 60-second video starts (audio off, captions on). It shows a real restaurant dashboard with blurred-out details.

**What you see in those 60 seconds:**
- 47 Google reviews (up from 12)
- Social posts for the entire week already published
- Review responses that sound warm and human
- One metric: "$8,400 revenue from AI-driven reservations this month"

Below the video: "This is what your marketing looks like when an AI does it for you. **$99/month. Start your 14-day trial.**"

No mystique. No "Book a Demo." No hiding the price like you're ashamed of it. You either want this or you don't. We're not interested in tire-kickers.

### Brand Voice: Confident, Not Cute
This is a product for people who work 80-hour weeks. They don't want personality — they want performance.

**Voice principles:**
- **Short sentences.** No fluff.
- **Active voice.** "Sous responds to every review" not "Reviews are responded to."
- **Specific numbers.** "47 reviews in 90 days" not "more reviews."
- **No jargon.** No "leverage," no "optimize," no "synergy."

Think Hemingway, not HubSpot. Say what it does. Show the results. Get out of the way.

### What to Say NO To
**NO to free trials without credit cards.** If they won't risk $99, they're not serious. We want intent, not emails.

**NO to three pricing tiers.** There's one product. It costs $99/month. Period. The "Starter/Pro/Enterprise" game is for companies that don't know what they're selling. We do.

**NO to "AI-powered" in the copy.** Of course it's AI. The iPhone doesn't say "computer-powered." Stop talking about the technology. Talk about the result.

**NO to testimonials as separate sections.** Weave them into the story. Show the review increase graph, then the restaurant owner saying "I couldn't believe it." Make proof inseparable from narrative.

**NO to "Request a Demo."** That's for enterprise bloatware that takes six months to implement. This takes six minutes.

### The Emotional Hook: You're Not Alone Anymore
Every restaurant owner has this moment at 11 PM after closing: "I should post something on Instagram. I should respond to reviews. I should... I should..." Then they pour a drink and feel guilty.

**Sous is the end of that guilt.**

The hook isn't productivity. It's *relief*. It's the feeling when someone you trust says "I've got this" and you actually believe them. That's what we're selling.

The tagline isn't "AI Marketing Automation." It's **"Your marketing, handled."**

Two words. Complete transformation. When someone asks what Sous does, that's the answer. When they see the dashboard, they *feel* the answer.
