## What Didn't Work

**Process violated before value delivered.**
- Wave 1 shipped pricing page for product with no moat, no distribution, no retention.
- Beautiful execution of flawed strategy = expensive learning.
- Should have entered HOLD state at Round 2, not post-board.

**No customer voice in room.**
- 15 documents. Zero restaurant owner interviews.
- Positioning assumptions untested. "Sous" never validated with actual users.
- Demo script (11 PM guilt) built on empathy, not data.

**Technical risks catalogued but not prioritized.**
- Risks #10-24 added *after* decisions locked. Risk theater, not risk management.
- CDN, Loom embeds, PostHog — all downstream dependencies identified late.
- Should precede synthesis, not follow it.

**Distribution treated as footnote.**
- Elon flagged "ZERO distribution strategy" Round 1.
- Decisions doc deferred to "Open Questions."
- Board still found gap. Unforced error.

**Retention insight came too late.**
- Shonda's roadmap (362 lines, brilliant) written *after* Wave 1 shipped.
- Daily hooks, IQ score, peer benchmarking — core retention — absent from V1 plan.
- Relief vs. addiction distinction should have gated go/no-go.

---

## What Agency Should Do Differently

**Strategic validation before tactical build.**
- Jensen's 3 questions (data moat, integrations, AI 10x) should precede PRD, not follow board review.
- Buffett's economics check (CAC, cost-to-serve, LTV) should block synthesis if unknown.
- No pricing page until business model defensible.

**Customer voice in Round 1.**
- 5 restaurant owner interviews before naming product.
- Test "Sous" vs. "LocalGenius" with users who'd pay.
- Validate 11 PM guilt hook with actual guilt-havers.

**Risk assessment precedes decisions.**
- Technical feasibility scan (Loom, CDN, PostHog) before locking architecture.
- Distribution channel hypothesis before promising 25 customers in 90 days.
- Retention mechanics sketched before defining product scope.

**Board review at decision lock, not after ship.**
- Board sees synthesis *before* Wave 1 starts.
- HOLD verdict saves build time, not just prevents Wave 2.
- Jensen/Buffett/Shonda gaps surface when fixable, not after committed.

**Retention = Product, not Feature.**
- Shonda's flywheel (daily hooks, IQ score, revenue attribution) should be V1 scope.
- Relief without retention = one-time transaction.
- Addiction mechanics define moat. Ship both or ship neither.

---

## Key Learning

**Speed without strategy compounds the wrong thing.**

Elon's "ship in 3 days" met Steve's craft standards, but neither asked: should we ship this at all?

Board caught the gap. Retrospective confirms: beautiful execution of commodity strategy = costly distraction masquerading as momentum.
