# Round 1: Steve Jobs — Chief Design & Brand Officer

## Product Name: **Preflight**

Not "WP Deploy Guard." That's a committee name. It's descriptive, forgettable, and screams "enterprise checkbox software."

**Preflight.** One word. Everyone knows what it means. Pilots don't take off without it. Every WordPress user who's lost a weekend to a broken update will *feel* this name in their gut. It's a verb and a noun. It's a ritual. It's confidence.

---

## Design Philosophy: Invisible Until It Matters

The best software is like a great referee — you forget they're there until something goes wrong.

Preflight should add *zero cognitive load* to the happy path. When everything's fine, it's a green light and you're done. No dashboards to study. No 47-tab settings page. No "would you like to configure your pre-update verification threshold parameters?"

We're not building a cockpit. We're building a seatbelt. You put it on, you forget it's there, and when you hit something — it saves your life.

---

## First 30 Seconds: The "Oh, That's It?" Moment

Install. Activate. Done.

The user presses "Update" on a plugin — and for the first time *ever*, a small, elegant panel slides in: three green checkmarks, one amber note ("This update changes 47 files"), and a single blue button: **"Apply Update."**

That's it. No tour. No onboarding wizard. No "please create an account." The product explains itself by *being used*. The user thinks: "Where has this been my whole life?"

If they need to rollback, one click. Not buried in settings. Not "are you sure? are you really sure?" Just: **Undo.** Like pressing Cmd+Z on a document. That simple.

---

## Brand Voice: Calm Confidence

Preflight doesn't shout. It doesn't use exclamation points. It speaks like a senior engineer who's seen everything and panics about nothing.

- Not: "WARNING! CRITICAL ISSUE DETECTED!"
- Yes: "This update requires PHP 8.1. Your host runs 8.0. Update blocked."

No jargon. No hedging. No "potentially" or "may cause." Direct, factual, human. The plugin knows what's wrong and tells you. Period.

---

## What We Say NO To

- **No external accounts.** The moment you ask someone to sign up, you've lost them. Everything runs locally.
- **No premium upsell on safety features.** Rolling back your site isn't a "Pro" feature. That's like making airbags a paid option.
- **No visual regression in v1.** Headless browsers, Puppeteer, Chrome dependencies — it's complexity cancer. Ship without it. Let competitors add bloat. We ship lean.
- **No multi-site dashboard in v1.** Do one site perfectly before doing fifty poorly.
- **No "settings."** Every setting is a failure of design. The right defaults ship configured.

---

## The Emotional Hook: Control Returned

Here's the truth nobody says out loud: WordPress users live in fear. Fear of clicking "Update All." Fear of auto-updates happening at 3am. Fear that today's the day their client's site goes white-screen.

Preflight gives them their **power back.** It transforms "update and pray" into "update and know."

That's not a feature. That's *relief.* That's sleeping through the night. That's telling your client "yes, it's safe" and *meaning it.*

People don't love tools. They love how tools make them feel. Preflight makes them feel like professionals in control of their craft — not gamblers hoping the slot machine pays out.

---

**Ship it. Keep it simple. Make it feel like magic.**
