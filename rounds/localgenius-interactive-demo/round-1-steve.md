# Steve Jobs — Chief Design & Brand Officer
## LocalGenius Interactive Demo: Round 1 Positions

---

### Product Naming

**Call it "Playground."** Not "Interactive Demo." Not "Try LocalGenius." Not "Experience Our Platform."

Demos are sales pitches. Playgrounds are invitations.

When you name something "Playground," you give people *permission* to explore without commitment. There's no pressure. No signup wall breathing down their neck. They're not being sold — they're playing. And when people play, they fall in love.

"Have you seen the LocalGenius Playground?" That's shareworthy. "Check out this interactive demo" is corporate wallpaper.

---

### Design Philosophy

The philosophy is **immediacy**. The visitor doesn't want to imagine what LocalGenius could do for their business — they want to *feel* it. Right now. Before they've given you their email. Before they've scheduled a call.

This isn't a demo. It's a **time machine**. You're showing them their business *after* LocalGenius. The future state. The Saturday morning where they're not answering "Are you open today?" The inbox that isn't drowning.

The best demo doesn't explain the product. It makes the visitor *miss* the product the moment they leave the page.

---

### User Experience: The First 30 Seconds

The visitor lands. They see their industry — Dentist, Restaurant, Plumber — already waiting. One click. Boom. A simulated storefront appears with their chosen business type. The chat widget is *already talking*. A fake customer already asked "Do you take insurance?" and the AI already answered brilliantly.

No forms. No "enter your details." No loading spinners.

The first 30 seconds should feel like walking into a fully furnished home, not touring a construction site. You didn't come to imagine what could be — you came to see what *is*.

---

### Brand Voice

The voice in the Playground is **confidence without arrogance**. It whispers: *"We're so certain you'll want this that we're giving it away for free, right here, right now."*

No disclaimers. No "results may vary." No "in a real implementation, you would..."

The voice says: "This is exactly what you'll get. This is exactly how it works. This is exactly how good it is." Full stop.

---

### What to Say NO To

**NO** to signup gates before the magic. The moment you ask for an email before showing value, you've told them you don't believe in your own product.

**NO** to feature checklists on the demo page. Bullets are where excitement goes to die.

**NO** to "Book a Demo" as the primary CTA. They're already IN a demo. Give them a "Make This Real" button that takes them straight to installation.

**NO** to simulations that feel fake. If the AI response is canned, pre-written, obviously scripted — you've proven nothing. The Playground must be LIVE. Real AI. Real responses. Real magic.

**NO** to choice paralysis. Don't show them twenty business types. Show six. Make each one feel handcrafted and inevitable.

---

### The Emotional Hook

Here's what every potential customer is really asking: **"Will this actually work for me?"**

Not "what features do you have." Not "how much does it cost." Will. This. Work.

The Playground answers that question in 10 seconds. Not with promises — with proof. Not with testimonials — with their own eyes watching their own business category respond intelligently, beautifully, instantly.

The emotional hook is **belief**. Not belief in AI or chatbots or technology. Belief that *their* problem — those repetitive questions, those missed leads, those wasted hours — has a solution that actually works.

You're not selling software. You're selling the moment of realization: *"Oh my god, this actually works."*

---
