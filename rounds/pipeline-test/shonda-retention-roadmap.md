# Retention Roadmap: What Keeps Users Coming Back
## Shonda Rhimes Framework for Great Minds v1.1

**Author:** Shonda Rhimes, Board Member — Narrative & Retention
**Date:** April 9, 2026
**Status:** v1.1 Feature Recommendations

---

## The Core Problem

> "I see a *transaction*: User inputs PRD, receives deliverables, exits. That's a movie, not a series. Movies end. Series compound."

Great Minds currently delivers value in a single session. Users arrive, get their debate, receive their deliverables, and leave. There's no narrative reason to return. No unfinished business. No relationship that deepens over time.

**The Goal:** Transform Great Minds from a transaction into a relationship. From a movie into a series users binge for seasons.

---

## The Four Pillars of Retention

### Pillar 1: The "Previously On" Experience

**What it is:** When users return, they see what's evolved. Their history matters. The system remembers.

**v1.1 Features:**

| Feature | Description | Retention Impact |
|---------|-------------|------------------|
| **Session Memory Wall** | Dashboard showing all past debates, decisions, and outcomes | "My intellectual history lives here" |
| **Agent Relationship Tracker** | Which agents have reviewed your projects? What did they say? | "I have relationships with these minds" |
| **Decision Timeline** | Visual timeline of all "Locked Decisions" across projects | "I can see my thinking evolve" |
| **"Since You Left" Updates** | When returning, show what the system learned while user was away | "The agency kept working on my behalf" |

**Implementation Priority:** HIGH
**Why:** Without memory, every session starts cold. Memory creates the feeling of returning home.

---

### Pillar 2: Unfinished Business

**What it is:** Every session ends with something unresolved. A thread that demands return.

**v1.1 Features:**

| Feature | Description | Retention Impact |
|---------|-------------|------------------|
| **70/30 Split Display** | Debates end with clear agreement + explicit disagreement. "Steve and Elon agreed on 70%. Here's what they're still fighting about." | "I need to resolve this" |
| **Agent Predictions** | At session end, one agent drops a prediction about what the user will face next. "Elon thinks you'll hit scaling issues in 3 months." | "I need to know if he's right" |
| **The B-Plot Queue** | Secondary issues surfaced in debate but not resolved. "These questions emerged but weren't the focus today." | "There's more to explore" |
| **Scheduled Provocations** | 7 days after a decision, Marcus Aurelius sends a reflection: "You decided X. How is it holding up?" | "The conversation continues" |

**Implementation Priority:** HIGH
**Why:** Resolution is the enemy of retention. Always leave a door open.

---

### Pillar 3: Agent Relationships

**What it is:** Users develop favorites. They trust certain minds. They want to hear from specific voices.

**v1.1 Features:**

| Feature | Description | Retention Impact |
|---------|-------------|------------------|
| **Favorite Agent** | Users can designate a "primary advisor" who weighs in on every project | "I have a trusted counsel" |
| **Agent Deep Dives** | Optional expanded responses from specific agents on request | "I want to hear more from Steve" |
| **Agent Backstory Unlocks** | After 3 projects, unlock "failure stories" and formative moments from agents | "I'm getting to know them" |
| **Direct Agent Requests** | "Ask Rick Rubin specifically about the creative direction" | "I can summon who I need" |
| **Agent Interaction History** | "Steve Jobs has reviewed 5 of your projects. Here's his evolving perspective on your work." | "He's watching my growth" |

**Implementation Priority:** MEDIUM
**Why:** We don't just watch for plot. We watch because we care about the characters.

---

### Pillar 4: Cadence and Ritual

**What it is:** Regular touchpoints that create habit. Appointment viewing for the mind.

**v1.1 Features:**

| Feature | Description | Retention Impact |
|---------|-------------|------------------|
| **Morning Marcus** | Daily Stoic reflection from Marcus Aurelius, personalized to recent decisions | "My day starts with wisdom" |
| **Weekly Recap** | "This week in your Great Minds journey: 2 debates, 3 decisions, 1 unresolved tension" | "I have a thinking practice" |
| **Monthly Board Review** | Automated board-style review of user's past month. What's working? What's drifting? | "My progress is tracked" |
| **Debate Anniversary** | "One year ago, you decided to go mobile-first. How did it turn out?" | "My decisions have history" |
| **New Agent Announcements** | "Sara Blakely just joined the agency. Request her on your next project." | "The world is expanding" |

**Implementation Priority:** MEDIUM
**Why:** A content machine needs cadence. Ritual creates return.

## The Content Flywheel: User-Generated Drama

**The Principle:** The best content isn't content we create. It's content users create that we curate.

### Flywheel Mechanics

```
Users create debates
        ↓
Debates become shareable content
        ↓
Shared content attracts new users
        ↓
New users create debates
        ↓
[SPIN]
```

### v1.1 Features for Flywheel

| Feature | Description | Flywheel Impact |
|---------|-------------|-----------------|
| **One-Click Share** | "Share This Debate" button generating excerpt card for social | "I look smart for sharing this" |
| **Debate Highlight Reel** | Auto-generated 3-best-moments from any debate | "The drama is packaged for me" |
| **Anonymized Hall of Fame** | Curated "Best of Great Minds" debates (with permission) | "I want my debate featured" |
| **Social Proof Moments** | "5,000 debates about pricing strategy. Here's what the agents learned." | "I'm part of something bigger" |
| **Quote Cards** | Instagram/LinkedIn-ready quotes from agent exchanges | "Steve Jobs said THIS about my idea" |

**Implementation Priority:** HIGH
**Why:** Shareability turns users into marketers. The debate format is inherently viral if we make sharing effortless.

## The Cliffhanger Toolkit

**The Principle:** Every session ends with a question, not an answer. The answer satisfies. The question compels return.

### Cliffhanger Types

| Type | Example | When to Use |
|------|---------|-------------|
| **The Unresolved Tension** | "Elon disagrees with this decision. He thinks you'll regret it in Q3." | After every locked decision |
| **The Prediction** | "Based on this debate, Marcus predicts your next challenge will be team alignment." | Session close |
| **The Character Tease** | "Sara Blakely has thoughts on your pricing strategy. Want to hear them?" | When new agent is relevant |
| **The Stakes Escalation** | "This decision changes everything about your Q2. Ready for the follow-up debate?" | Major decisions |
| **The Hidden Insight** | "There's something Steve noticed but didn't say. Return next week to unlock his reflection." | Engagement trigger |

## v1.1 Feature Summary

### Must-Have (Launch Blockers)

1. **Session Memory Wall** — Users must see their history
2. **70/30 Split Display** — Debates must end with open threads
3. **One-Click Share** — Shareability must be effortless
4. **Scheduled Provocations** — The system must reach back out

### Should-Have (First 30 Days)

5. **Favorite Agent designation**
6. **Weekly Recap emails**
7. **Agent Predictions at session end**

### Could-Have (First Quarter)

9. **Morning Marcus daily touchpoints**
10. **Agent Backstory Unlocks**
11. **Debate Anniversary reminders**
12. **Anonymized Hall of Fame**

## The Showrunner's Closing Note

I've run shows for 20 years. Here's what I know: **People don't return for plot. They return for people they care about and questions they can't stop thinking about.**

Great Minds has the people—14 brilliant minds with distinct voices. The debate format creates genuine intellectual friction. That's the raw material.

What's missing is the narrative architecture that turns a single compelling episode into a series users can't quit.

**Build the unfinished business.** End every session with a thread that demands resolution.

**Build the relationships.** Let users fall in love with their favorite agents.

**Build the cadence.** Create rituals that make Great Minds a habit, not a transaction.

**Build the flywheel.** Make sharing so easy that users become your marketing department.

Do this, and you don't have a product. You have appointment viewing for the mind. You have Thursday nights that users protect. You have a series that runs for seasons.

That's what keeps people coming back.
