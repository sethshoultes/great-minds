# Steve Jobs — Design & Brand Review

## Product Naming

**ASSEMBLE.**

Drop the rest. "Agents Assemble" sounds like a Marvel movie for developers who still think they're cool. One word. Verb. Command. *Assemble.* It's what you do. It's an order. When someone asks "What did you use?" the answer is "Assemble." Clean. Impossible to forget.

## Design Philosophy

This workshop is about **automation as autonomy**. Not "AI will do your work" — that's lazy thinking. This is about building a *system* that thinks while you dream. The Ralph Wiggum Loop isn't a joke — it's the most honest name for what every developer secretly wants: a tireless worker who doesn't need hand-holding.

What makes this insanely great? **The exercises are the product.** Not slides. Not scripts. Seven copy-paste moments where someone goes from "I don't get it" to "I will never work the same way again." The slides are just permission to start typing.

## User Experience — The First 30 Seconds

You open Exercise 1. You paste one line. You watch Claude commit code *without you touching anything.* Your jaw drops. That's it. That's the first 30 seconds.

No theory. No "let me explain what an agent is." You *feel* the power before you understand it. Like the first time you pinch-zoomed on an iPhone — you didn't need a tutorial. You just *knew*.

## Brand Voice

**Casual expertise.** Talk like a senior engineer at a whiteboard, not a conference speaker with a clicker. Say "Ralph Wiggum Loop" out loud — that's the voice. Self-aware. A little absurd. Deeply competent underneath.

Never say "leverage." Never say "utilize." Never say "empower your workflow." Say "build while you sleep." Say "ship it." Say "your turn."

## What to Say NO To

- **No "Introduction to AI Agents" section.** If they're in this room, they know. Respect their time.
- **No optional exercises.** Seven exercises. All required. If one is skippable, delete it.
- **No expected output longer than 5 lines.** If you're showing them paragraphs, you've already lost them.
- **No second chances on placeholder content.** The PRD is right to make this a hard blocker. Ship complete or don't ship.
- **No apologizing for "simplicity."** The Ralph Wiggum Loop is 10 lines. That's not simple — that's *distilled*.

## The Emotional Hook

Here's why people will love this:

**You will leave this room dangerous.**

Not "informed." Not "enabled." *Dangerous.* You'll know how to spin up agents that debate each other, that build features while you eat dinner, that catch bugs before you wake up. Your competitors are still reading blog posts about AI. You're building armies.

The hook isn't productivity. The hook is *power*. The feeling that you've unlocked something that changes the game. That's what the first iPhone felt like. That's what this workshop delivers — in 30 minutes.
