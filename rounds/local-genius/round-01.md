# LocalGenius — Round 1: Initial Positions

**Phase**: Position (Rounds 1-3)
**Date**: 2026-04-01

## [STEVE] ROUND 1 — INITIAL POSITION

### 1. Product Design Vision

LocalGenius is not a dashboard. Not a tool suite. Not a SaaS product with a nav bar full of tabs. It is a **trusted employee**.

The entire product lives in **one conversation thread**. No settings page. No "go to the social tab." The owner opens the app, sees their thread — past activity, upcoming actions, recent results — and types or speaks what they need.

Visual language: warm, confident, local. Earth tones. Generous whitespace. Real business photos. The feeling of a great local hardware store where the person behind the counter knows your name.

**Onboarding must be magical.** Five minutes. Owner shares business name, what they do, location, uploads photos. LocalGenius returns: a website, Google Business Profile draft, a social post ready to publish, and a suggested campaign. That moment — seeing their business look *professional* for the first time — is the iPhone moment.

The Weekly Digest is the product's heartbeat. Not a data dump — a *story*. "Here's what happened. Here's what I did. Here's what I recommend."

**Single design principle: make the owner feel like they finally have someone in their corner.**

### 2. Product/Market Fit Analysis

The fit is real. The market has given owners more tools, more logins, more dashboards. That treats the symptom (lack of skill) instead of the disease (lack of time). LocalGenius treats the disease.

The $20B TAM framing is lazy. The real question: how quickly can we deliver a win so convincingly that owners tell everyone they know? Growth engine = word of mouth at the local level.

**Start vertical. Pick restaurants.** Visual, social, review-driven, content-starved. Win them so completely that the category becomes synonymous with our brand.

### 3. Customer Personas

**Maria — The Dentist Who Hates Marketing** (41, Phoenix, two-chair practice, $400K revenue). Fears looking unprofessional. Dreams of someone who just handles it. Her fervor as a convert makes her our best advocate.

**Darnell — The Second-Generation Barbershop Owner** (34, Atlanta). Inherited his father's shop. Every tool he's tried felt built for a startup, not a barbershop. Earns trust when we say "23 people found your shop" instead of "engagement metrics."

**Linda — The Franchise Operator** (52, seven Supercuts). Not core v1 but the product must be architected for her from day one — that's where economics get interesting.

### 4. Team Personas

The wrong team: talented engineers who've never worked at a local business. The right team has felt what it's like to be a small business owner.

- **Head of Product**: Square or Toast background
- **Design Lead**: Consumer product, thinks about the first 30 seconds
- **AI Lead**: Obsessed with reliability and trust, not just capability
- **Customer Success Lead**: Genuinely loves small businesses

Small. Focused. Every person an A-player.

### 5. Marketing Goals

10,000 users is a metric, not a goal. The real goal: **create a category**.

1. Win one city completely (Austin, 500 businesses)
2. Create ten stories that spread on their own (local Facebook groups, Chambers of Commerce)
3. Build presence inside local business infrastructure (SBA, SCORE)
4. Make the product itself the marketing (shareable Weekly Digest, subtle "Posted by LocalGenius" signature)

**NPS above 60** (raising the PRD's 50).

### 6. Marketing Messaging

**The Big Idea:** The employee you always needed but could never afford.

**Tagline: "Your business, handled."**

Three words. Doesn't say "AI." Doesn't say "marketing." Says: the thing keeping you up at night, handled.

**The single emotion: relief.**

**Words we never use:** "AI-powered," "all-in-one platform," "streamline your workflow," "solution."

## [ELON] ROUND 1 — INITIAL POSITION

### 1. Product Design Vision

What is this product actually doing? A **multi-channel execution layer with a natural language interface on top**. Every feature is an API integration plus thin AI orchestration. The tech is not the hard part. The hard parts: **cold-start problem** and **trust problem**.

**Do NOT build a website builder.** Commoditized to zero. Integrate with existing hosting, use templates, get indexed in 48 hours. Perfection is the enemy of published.

**Core architecture loop:**
1. Owner input (voice/text)
2. AI interprets intent
3. System drafts content/action
4. Owner approves (one tap) or auto-posts if pre-authorized
5. Confirmation + metrics

An **agentic loop with a human approval gate**. Every feature maps onto this. Resist building 12 screens.

### 2. Product/Market Fit Analysis

The unit economics:
- LTV/CAC > 3:1. At $79/month, 24-month retention → LTV ~$1,900. CAC under $600.
- AI costs under 15% = $11.85/user/month. Achievable with smart caching.
- **Retention is the kill shot.** SMB churn is 5-10%/month. Product must actively make them money.

**Start restaurants.** Highest content frequency, reviews = revenue, extreme word-of-mouth. Not optional — it's physics.

### 3. Customer Personas

**Maria, Restaurant Owner** ($29 tier) — 45-55, $300-800K revenue. Decision trigger: one story from a trusted peer.

**Dave, Franchise Operator** ($79 tier) — 35-50, 3-8 locations, $2-10M revenue. Currently paying agency $3-8K/month. **Highest-value segment** — 1 contract = 5-50 locations.

**Kevin, the Plumber** (churn risk) — 30-45, solo operator. Will cancel in 90 days unless you show lead attribution. Must track leads to his phone number.

### 4. Team Personas

**6 people. 6 months. v1.**

1. Technical Co-Founder / Lead Engineer
2. AI/Prompt Engineer (practitioner, not researcher)
3. Mobile Engineer (React Native or Flutter)
4. Product Designer (UX + UI, one person)
5. Growth / Go-to-Market Lead (B2SMB, comfortable at restaurant association meetings)
6. Founder/CEO (sells, fundraises, does everything else)

**Do NOT hire first 6 months:** sales team, marketing team, customer success, data scientist.

### 5. Marketing Goals

1. **500 paying users in 90 days** (restaurant vertical) — understand retention before scaling
2. **30-day retention above 80%** — North Star for first 6 months
3. **NPS > 50 within 6 months**
4. **3 case studies with measurable ROI by month 4** — hard data, not stories
5. **CAC under $150** for restaurant vertical

Path: 500 restaurants → expand to salons/dental by month 4-6 → paid acquisition months 7-12.

**Do NOT run paid ads in month 1.** You don't know your retention yet.

### 6. Marketing Messaging

**Core value prop:** It's the first hour of your week back.

**Primary hook:** "Your competitor across the street is getting found on Google. You're not. Here's why, and here's how to fix it in 5 minutes." — Fear + specificity + low barrier.

**Secondary:** "Talk to it like an employee. It does the rest."

**Three questions every message must answer:**
1. Will this get me more customers?
2. How much time will I spend on it?
3. Can I trust it not to embarrass me publicly?
