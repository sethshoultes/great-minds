# Steve Jobs — Round 2 Response

## Challenging Elon's Weakest Positions

**"Form intake only, no chat."** This is optimizing for the wrong metric.

Elon wants to ship faster. I want people to *feel* something. A form is a transaction. A conversation is a relationship. Yes, chat takes longer to build. But the business owner sitting at their kitchen table at 11pm doesn't want to fill out a form — they want to feel like someone's listening. That's not polish. That's the *entire product*.

You can't iterate your way to magic. You either have it or you don't.

**"Let the vertical dictate the palette. Dentist = blue/white."** This is the Squarespace trap.

Every dentist already looks like every other dentist. That's not a solution — that's the problem we're solving. When we extract color from *their* photos, *their* office, *their* actual business — that's when they see themselves in it. That 2-5 seconds of image analysis isn't latency. It's the moment we prove we're paying attention.

**B2B2C as the primary growth engine.** You're chasing scale before you have soul.

White-labeling to agencies means losing control of the first impression. The agency marks it up, strips the name, owns the relationship. You get volume. You lose *love*. The customers never know what they experienced. That's not a brand — it's a commodity.

---

## Defending My Positions

**Why does design quality matter in v1?**

Because there is no v2 for the customer who bounces in disgust. First impressions are permanent. We're not building a prototype to learn from — we're building a product that represents someone's *business*. Their livelihood. Their dream. Ship something ugly and fast, and they won't come back to see how much you've improved.

The person we're building for has been burned before. By their nephew. By Wix. By a freelancer who ghosted them. Trust is the scarcest resource. We earn it with quality, not speed.

---

## Concessions — Where Elon Is Right

**Custom domains in v1: Cut it.** He's right. Subdomains ship faster and eliminate a category of support nightmares. The magic happens before the URL.

**External API lookups (Google, Yelp): Make them optional.** Don't block the experience on third-party rate limits. Enrichment happens after the preview, not before.

**Stateless regeneration for revisions.** This is elegant. No patching, no edge cases. Each revision is a fresh build. I concede this is architecturally correct.

**< 30 seconds for preview is the right target.** Not five minutes. Thirty seconds. He's right that we should be embarrassed by anything slower.

---

## My Non-Negotiables (Locked)

1. **Conversational intake, not a form.** The first interaction must feel human. This is the product.

2. **We choose the template — no gallery.** Asking the customer to pick is asking them to do our job. We demonstrate taste.

3. **Color extraction from their photos.** This is how we prove we *saw* them. Generic palettes are a betrayal.

---

*Speed without soul is just efficient mediocrity.*

*We ship fast. But we ship something worth using.*
