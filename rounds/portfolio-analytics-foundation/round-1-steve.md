# Steve Jobs — Chief Design & Brand Officer

## Product Naming

**Compass.**

One word. Memorable. It's what you use when you're lost. Your portfolio is five ships sailing in different directions with no idea where they are. A compass doesn't show you everything — it shows you **direction**. North. Growth. True north.

Not "Portfolio Analytics Dashboard" or "featureDream Insights" or some enterprise garbage. Compass.

## Design Philosophy

**Ruthless clarity.**

Analytics tools are bloated disasters. They give you 400 metrics when you need 4. They show you everything, which means they show you nothing.

Compass shows you ONE screen. Like the iPhone lock screen, everything critical is visible in 3 seconds. DAU. Retention curves. Which product is dying. Which is thriving. That's it.

The dashboard is not customizable. No drag-and-drop widgets. No "personalized views." We decide what matters. The designer's job is to have an opinion.

## User Experience: The First 30 Seconds

You open Compass. **Instantly**, you see five columns — one per product. Color-coded health: green (growing), yellow (stable), red (bleeding).

Each column shows THREE numbers:
1. Daily active users (the truth)
2. 7-day retention (the future)
3. Revenue per user (the value)

That's it. You know in 10 seconds which product needs your attention today.

No loading spinners. No "analyzing..." No charts that require a PhD to interpret. Just truth, instantly.

Like checking your pulse. You don't need a 40-page medical report. You need to know: am I alive?

## Brand Voice

**Direct. Honest. Unsentimental.**

Compass doesn't say "Your engagement metrics are trending positively!" It says: **"Pinned is dying. 62% of users never came back."**

It doesn't celebrate vanity metrics. It doesn't sugarcoat. It's the friend who tells you that you have spinach in your teeth.

The voice is conversational but never casual. Precise but never robotic. Think: a great coach. They don't coddle you. They tell you the truth because they want you to win.

## What to Say NO To

**NO** to 47 different chart types. Line charts for trends. Bar charts for comparisons. That's it.

**NO** to customizable dashboards. Everyone gets the same view. If you need something different, you're asking the wrong question.

**NO** to real-time updates. Hourly refresh is enough. Obsessing over minute-by-minute metrics is how you lose your mind.

**NO** to user settings. No light/dark mode toggles. No font size pickers. We designed it. It's right. Use it.

**NO** to "insights" generated by AI. GPT-4 will tell you "engagement is down 12% due to seasonal factors." That's fortune-telling, not analytics. Show the number. Let the human think.

## The Emotional Hook

**You will stop guessing.**

Right now, you're flying blind. Every decision is a coin flip. "Should I invest in Dash or Pinned?" You don't know. You *feel* like Dash is doing well, but feelings lie.

Compass makes you **certain**. You open it, and you *know*. LocalGenius has 4x the LTV of Shipyard. Great Minds costs $200 in tokens per shipped project. Retention on Pinned dropped 30% after the last update.

That feeling — the feeling of **knowing** — is intoxicating. It's the difference between wandering in the dark and turning on the lights.

People don't love analytics. They love **control**. Compass gives you control.

## The Underlying Truth

This isn't an analytics product. It's a **decision-making product**.

The best products don't give you more information. They give you **clarity**. They remove the noise. They show you what matters.

A thermometer doesn't show you air pressure, humidity, wind speed, and dew point. It shows you **temperature**. That's what you need.

Compass is a thermometer for your portfolio. Is it healthy? Is it sick? Where's the fever?

You open it every morning. Three seconds. You know exactly what to do.

That's the product.
