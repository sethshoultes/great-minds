## Process Metrics
- Total rounds: 10 (2 debate + 1 plan + 5 build + 1 review + 1 ship)
- Total output: ~195KB across 8 deliverables
- Sub-agents spawned: 6 (3 by Steve, 3 by Elon)
- Cross-doc issues caught in review: 14 (all resolved)
- Locked decisions amended mid-process: 1 (500 → 300/500 target)

## What Worked
1. **Debate-then-build model** — 2 debate rounds was enough to align. More would have been diminishing returns.
2. **Parallel build tracks** — Steve's creative team and Elon's analytical team running simultaneously cut build time roughly in half.
3. **James Park (creative reviewer)** — Cross-document QA caught persona drift and vocabulary leaks. Essential role.
4. **Marcus as moderator** — Kept agents on track without micromanaging. Synthesized tensions rather than picking sides.
5. **Self-correcting targets** — Elon's math disproved the original target. Agency amended rather than defended.

## What to Improve
1. **Canonical persona definitions before parallel build** — Maria was a dentist in one doc, restaurant owner in another. Establish shared facts first.
2. **Drafts → finals pipeline** — agents wrote to drafts/ then Marcus moved to final/. Could be smoother.
3. **Jensen should review during build, not after** — his strategic perspective would have caught timing issues earlier.

**How to apply:** On next project, establish canonical facts (personas, key terms) as a shared document before dispatching parallel build agents.
