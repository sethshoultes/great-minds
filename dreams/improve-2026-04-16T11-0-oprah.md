## Executive Summary

I've spent 37,000 interviews learning one thing: **every person wants to feel seen, heard, and capable.** That's not just a talk show insight — it's the lens through which I review every first-time user experience.

Here's the truth: your products are built by experts, for experts. And that's a problem. Because the person opening your product for the first time isn't an expert. They're someone hoping this tool will solve a problem they couldn't solve themselves. They're vulnerable. They're hopeful. And in the first five minutes, you either make them feel smart or you make them feel dumb.

Too often, right now, your products make people feel dumb.

## Product-by-Product First-5-Minutes Analysis

### LocalGenius — "You didn't open a restaurant to do marketing"
**The Promise:** Beautiful. That headline speaks directly to the overwhelmed restaurant owner. It says: "I see you. I understand."

**The First 5 Minutes:**
- Landing page delivers on emotional resonance ✓
- "Set up in 5 minutes" — clear expectation ✓
- Three-step onboarding requiring only business name — low friction ✓

**Where Confusion Creeps In:**
- **Pricing is hidden.** The business owner thinks: "This sounds too good to be true. What's the catch?" When you hide the price, you create anxiety instead of trust.
- **No social proof on the landing page.** Where are the real restaurant owners saying "this changed my life"? Numbers don't convince. Stories convince.
- **The "how" is missing.** You say you'll handle their marketing, but you don't show them what that looks like. A 30-second video of the dashboard would answer 80% of their unspoken questions.

**The Aunt at Thanksgiving Test:**
"It's like having a marketing person who works for you 24/7, but you never have to meet with them or explain what you need. You just say your restaurant name, and they handle everything — your social media, your reviews, your website."

👆 That explanation isn't on your landing page. It should be.

### Dash — WordPress Cmd+K Command Palette
**The Promise:** Power users will love this. But will they find it?

**The First 5 Minutes:**
- Plugin installs fine
- User goes to WordPress dashboard and... nothing has visibly changed
- **No onboarding. No welcome message. No "hey, try pressing Cmd+K!"**
- User has to already know Cmd+K exists to discover the product they just installed

**Where Confusion Happens:**
The product assumes you already understand what it is. That's fine for power users who sought it out. But what about the person who clicked "install" from a recommendation and then couldn't figure out what it does?

**What's Missing:**
- **A welcome screen** that appears once after activation: "Press ⌘K to supercharge your WordPress navigation. Here's what you can do..."
- **A persistent hint** (dismissible) in the admin bar until they've used it once
- **A tour** showing the three modes: search, commands (>), and users (@)

**The "Would Someone Tell Their Friend?" Test:**
Right now, someone might say "I installed this plugin but I don't know what it does." After a proper onboarding, they'd say "Oh my god, press Cmd+K in WordPress — it's life-changing."

### Pinned — WordPress Sticky Notes
**The Promise:** Team reminders where the team already is.

**The First 5 Minutes:**
- Plugin installs
- User sees... an empty dashboard widget
- **No notes, no guidance, no "double-click here to create your first note"**
- User thinks: "Is it working? Did it install correctly?"

**The Empty State Problem:**
An empty state is not a neutral state. An empty state says "you haven't done anything yet." A good empty state says "here's how to get started, and here's what it'll look like when you do."

**What's Missing:**
- **A sample note** that welcomes the user: "Welcome to Pinned! Double-click anywhere to create a note. Try it now! 👋 — The Pinned Team" (with instructions to delete it)
- **Tooltips** on the color swatches explaining what they're for
- **A "why" statement:** "Notes stay here even when Slack gets buried and email gets ignored"

**The "Real Person" Test:**
A busy WordPress admin who installs this between putting out fires needs to understand in 10 seconds: what is this, why should I care, and how do I use it. Right now, they get: a blank widget with no context.

### Great Minds Plugin — Claude Code Multi-Agent Agency
**The Promise:** PRD in, shipped product out. Revolutionary.

**The First 5 Minutes:**
- User runs `/agency-start`
- 30+ files are created
- User opens AGENTS.md, STATUS.md, MEMORY.md, SCOREBOARD.md...
- User thinks: "Where do I even begin?"

**Overwhelm is the Enemy:**
This product does something genuinely remarkable. But the first-time experience feels like being handed the cockpit of a 747 and told "have fun." The power is there. The guidance is not.

**What's Missing:**
- **A wizard, not a dump.** Instead of creating 30 files and saying "here you go," walk them through: "Step 1: Write your PRD. Step 2: Run /agency-debate. Step 3: Review the blueprint..."
- **A sample project** that's already half-complete so they can see what success looks like
- **A "your first ship" tutorial** that guides them through a tiny project (e.g., "Let's build a simple landing page in 15 minutes")
- **Progress indicators** — "You're on step 2 of 6 in the launch pipeline"

**The Emotional Reality:**
Someone who invests time learning Great Minds will be transformed. But 90% of people who try it will bounce in the first 5 minutes because they feel stupid. And they're not stupid — you just didn't meet them where they are.

### Shipyard AI — Autonomous Site Builder
**The Promise:** PRD in. Production out. Compelling.

**The First 5 Minutes:**
- User lands on the page
- Sees "7 agents" and "6-stage process" — interesting but abstract
- Scrolls down and sees token pricing — but what does 500K tokens mean in dollars? In time?
- **No visible portfolio. No case studies. No "here's what we built."**
- User thinks: "This sounds cool but... can you prove it?"

**The Trust Gap:**
You're asking someone to hand over their requirements and trust that a production-ready product will come out the other end. That's a big ask. The landing page doesn't earn that trust.

**What's Missing:**
- **Before/After case studies.** "Here's the PRD. Here's what we shipped. 5 days, $X."
- **A video walkthrough** of the process (even a 2-minute Loom)
- **Clear pricing in dollars**, not just tokens
- **A "try it" option** — a small free project, a demo PRD you can submit, something that lets them experience the magic before committing
- **Testimonials with faces and names** — not just quotes, real humans

## Cross-Product Patterns

### 1. Empty States Need Love
Across every product, empty states are treated as "nothing there yet" instead of opportunities to guide, educate, and encourage. Every empty state should answer: What is this? Why does it matter? What do I do next?

### 2. The Curse of Knowledge
Every product assumes users know more than they do. This is because *you* know your products intimately. But the first-time user doesn't have your context. They have three seconds of attention and a lifetime of products that disappointed them.

### 3. Missing Emotional Beats
Great onboarding has emotional beats:
1. **Recognition:** "I see the problem you have"
2. **Hope:** "This tool can solve it"
3. **Capability:** "Here's how easy it is"
4. **Victory:** "Look what you just did!"

Most of your products skip straight to the mechanics without hitting these emotional beats.

### 4. No Social Proof at the Moment of Decision
When users are deciding whether to invest time (or money) in your products, they're looking for proof. Other humans who took the risk and won. That proof is missing across the board.

## The First-5-Minutes Prescription

### For LocalGenius:
- Add pricing to the main page (transparency builds trust)
- Add 3 video testimonials from real restaurant owners
- Add a 45-second product tour video on the landing page

### For Dash:
- Add a one-time welcome modal after plugin activation
- Add a dismissible "Try ⌘K" prompt in the admin bar
- Create a 60-second "Getting Started" video

### For Pinned:
- Add a welcome note in the empty state
- Add tooltips explaining features on first use
- Add a "Why Pinned?" one-liner in the dashboard widget

### For Great Minds:
- Create a "Your First Ship" guided tutorial (15 minutes, tiny project)
- Add a step indicator showing pipeline progress
- Provide a sample completed project so users can see the destination

### For Shipyard AI:
- Add 3-5 portfolio case studies with before/after
- Convert token pricing to dollar ranges
- Add a 2-minute explainer video
- Add a "free micro-project" option to demonstrate capability

## The Question I Always Ask

At the end of every review, I ask: **"Would a real person actually use this?"**

Not a power user. Not a developer. Not someone who already believes. A real person — busy, skeptical, hopeful — who has 5 minutes and a problem to solve.

Right now, your products serve the believers. The converts. The people who already get it.

But the magic happens when you serve the skeptics. When someone who almost clicked away instead thinks: "Oh... I see. This is for me."

That's when you've built something that changes lives.

---

**Specific Actionable Recommendation:**
Pick one product — I'd suggest LocalGenius because it has the clearest commercial intent — and rebuild the first-5-minutes experience from scratch. Add a 60-second product tour video, 3 testimonials with faces and names, and transparent pricing. Test it with 5 people who've never seen the product before. Watch their faces. Listen to their questions. Then iterate.

*"You become what you believe."* If your onboarding makes users believe they can't figure it out, they won't. Make them believe they already have.
