## Product-by-Product Investment Analysis

### LocalGenius — AI Marketing for Local Businesses
**How Does It Make Money?**
Presumably subscription SaaS — but the pricing isn't visible on the site. This is like running a hamburger stand but hiding the menu behind the counter. If you're not sure what to charge, that's a sign you're not sure what the value is.

**My Guess at Unit Economics:**
- Target customer: Local restaurant or small business owner
- Likely willingness to pay: $50-200/month (based on comparable marketing automation tools)
- Customer acquisition cost: Unknown, but likely $100-500 per customer (paid ads, content marketing)
- Lifetime value: Dependent on churn — if they stay 12 months at $99/month, LTV is ~$1,200
- Rule of thumb: LTV should be 3x CAC minimum. Are you there? You need to measure this.

**The Moat Question:**
What stops Mailchimp, HubSpot, or any AI startup from doing exactly this but cheaper? Right now, the answer is: nothing, except you got there first. That's not a moat. That's a head start.

**The Investment Case:**
*Promising but unproven.* The market is real (local businesses hate marketing). The pain is real. But until I see pricing, CAC/LTV metrics, and churn rates, this is a hypothesis, not a business.

**Revenue Opportunity:**
- **Tiered pricing** based on business size or features (Basic/Pro/Agency)
- **Add-on services:** One-time website builds, logo design, menu photography
- **Multi-location enterprise tier:** $500-1000/month for restaurant groups
- **White-label licensing:** Let marketing agencies resell LocalGenius to their clients

### Dash — WordPress Cmd+K Command Palette
**How Does It Make Money?**
It doesn't. It's a free plugin.

**The Problem with Free:**
Free products are fantastic for building distribution and reputation. They're terrible as businesses. See's Candies doesn't give away chocolate and hope customers buy something else.

**The Investment Case:**
*Not investable as a standalone business.* But it could be:
- A lead magnet for paid products (Shipyard, LocalGenius)
- Part of a premium WordPress toolkit bundle
- Freemium with paid "power features"

**Revenue Opportunity:**
- **Pro version:** $49/year for custom hotkeys, command history, team sharing, analytics integration
- **Agency license:** $199/year for unlimited client sites
- **Integration partnerships:** Charge plugin vendors for premium placement in command results

### Pinned — WordPress Sticky Notes
**How Does It Make Money?**
It doesn't. Another free plugin.

**The Same Problem:**
Free is not a business model. Free is a distribution strategy. But distribution to where?

**The Investment Case:**
*Not investable as standalone.* Similar options as Dash.

**Revenue Opportunity:**
- **Pro version:** $39/year for unlimited notes, custom categories, Slack/Teams integration, note analytics
- **Team tier:** $99/year for advanced workflows, scheduled notes, role-based visibility

### Great Minds Plugin — Claude Code Multi-Agent Agency
**How Does It Make Money?**
It doesn't directly. It consumes API tokens (cost center) to produce shipped products (potential revenue).

**The Interesting Economics:**
This is a tool that builds things. The question isn't whether Great Minds makes money — it's whether the things Great Minds builds make money. It's a factory, not a retail store.

**The Investment Case:**
*Not investable as a product, but investable as infrastructure.* If Great Minds can reliably produce revenue-generating products at a positive ROI (revenue from shipped product > token cost to build it), then it's a lever, not a business.

**The Key Metric:**
**Revenue Per Token.** Track every project: how much did it cost in tokens? How much revenue did it generate? Over 50 projects, this ratio tells you everything.

**Revenue Opportunity:**
- This isn't a product to monetize directly — it's the engine that builds products to monetize
- The opportunity is **reducing the cost of building revenue-generating products**
- Focus: Ship products that actually charge money, then measure token ROI

### Shipyard AI — Autonomous Site Builder
**How Does It Make Money?**
Token-based project pricing. 500K-2M tokens for sites, 500K for plugins, 100K for revisions.

**This Is Actually a Business.**

**Estimated Unit Economics:**
- Project revenue: Let's assume 500K tokens = ~$500-1000 (rough estimate based on API costs + margin)
- Cost: Token costs are relatively fixed (API pricing). Labor cost is ~zero (it's autonomous)
- Gross margin: If charging $1000 for something that costs $200 in tokens, that's 80% gross margin. Excellent.
- CAC: Unknown but critical. How are you acquiring customers?
- LTV: One project, or do they come back? Revision packages suggest some retention, but is it enough?

**The Moat Question:**
What stops a competitor from offering the same thing? Not much, technically. But:
- Delivery reliability is a moat (100% delivery rate claim)
- Quality portfolio is a moat (if you build it)
- Reputation is a moat (if you earn it)
- Speed is a moat (if you're measurably faster)

**The Investment Case:**
*Investable with conditions.* This is a real service business with real margins. But I need to see:
1. Actual revenue numbers
2. Customer acquisition cost
3. Repeat purchase rate
4. Documented portfolio

**Revenue Opportunities:**
- **Retainer plans:** $500-1000/month for ongoing site maintenance and updates
- **Rush pricing:** 2x for 48-hour delivery
- **Hosting/maintenance:** $50-200/month recurring revenue post-delivery
- **Volume discounts:** Lock in agencies with 10-project bundles
- **White-label:** Let agencies resell Shipyard under their brand (30% premium)

Kill or maintain-only the products that don't make money. Put 100% energy into Shipyard and LocalGenius. "The difference between successful people and really successful people is that really successful people say no to almost everything."

## The Hard Numbers You Need to Track

1. **Revenue by product** — Monthly, quarterly
2. **Customer acquisition cost** — By channel, by product
3. **Lifetime value** — How long do customers stay? What do they spend over time?
4. **Gross margin** — Revenue minus direct costs (tokens, hosting)
5. **Revenue Per Token** — For Great Minds: (Revenue from shipped products) / (Total tokens consumed)
6. **Churn rate** — What % of customers leave each month?

*"Only when the tide goes out do you discover who's been swimming naked."* Right now, you don't have the metrics to know if you're swimming naked. Get the metrics.

Launch a pricing page for LocalGenius this week. Pick a price ($79/month), put it on the site, and measure conversion rate. A wrong price you can fix is better than a hidden price that tells you nothing. You need market signal, and you need it now.
