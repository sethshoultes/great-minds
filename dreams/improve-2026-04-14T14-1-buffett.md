### 5. Shipyard AI (www.shipyard.company)

**Current Revenue Model:** Project-based (token pricing)

**Revenue Assessment:**
- ✅ Already monetizing (clients pay for builds)
- ✅ Transparent pricing reduces sales friction
- ✅ 100% ship rate = good reputation
- ⚠️ Pure project revenue = lumpy cash flow
- ⚠️ No recurring revenue post-launch

**Revenue Opportunities:**

| Opportunity | Model | Est. Revenue/Client |
|-------------|-------|---------------------|
| Maintenance retainer | $99-299/month | $1,200-3,600/year |
| Analytics dashboard | $49/month | $588/year |
| Performance optimization | $199 one-time | $199 |
| Content updates | $99/month | $1,188/year |
| Priority rebuild queue | $299/month | $3,588/year |

**The Problem:** Shipyard is a services business disguised as a product. Services businesses are:
- Hard to scale
- Valued at 1-2x revenue (vs. 5-10x for SaaS)
- Dependent on capacity

**The Fix:** Turn Shipyard into a SaaS product:
- **Self-service tier:** Client uploads PRD, gets estimate, pays, receives site
- **Managed tier:** Same as current model with human review
- **Enterprise tier:** Dedicated capacity, SLA, account management

**Investability Score: 5/10**
- Generating revenue (good)
- Services model limits scale (bad)
- Path to product-market fit exists
