*featureDream IMPROVE Cycle — 2026-04-14*

## Executive Summary

Great products don't just acquire users—they **keep them coming back**. I'm evaluating this portfolio through the lens of retention: What's the daily hook? What's the weekly ritual? What makes users miss the product when it's gone?

---

## The Retention Framework

Before diving into products, here's how I think about retention hooks:

| Hook Type | Description | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Daily Habit** | Something users do every day | Checking email, social feed |
| **Weekly Ritual** | Predictable value on a schedule | Weekly digest, report, summary |
| **Milestone Moment** | Celebration of progress | "You shipped your 10th project!" |
| **Social Proof** | Others' activity that pulls you back | "3 team members acknowledged your note" |
| **Fear of Missing Out** | Something expires or changes | "Your note expires in 2 days" |
| **Ongoing Narrative** | Story that unfolds over time | Progress bars, streaks, journeys |

Now let's see which hooks exist—and which are missing.

## Product-by-Product Retention Analysis

### 1. LocalGenius (localgenius.company)

**Existing Hooks:**
- ✅ Weekly Monday digest (solid weekly ritual)
- ✅ Review notifications (reactive engagement)
- ✅ Social media posting schedule (content calendar creates anticipation)

**Missing Hooks:**
- ❌ No daily habit trigger
- ❌ No milestone celebrations
- ❌ No competitive/comparative elements
- ❌ No streak mechanics

**Retention Gap:** Users engage when notified but don't proactively return between notifications.

**Recommendations:**

| Hook | Implementation | Expected Impact |
|------|----------------|-----------------|
| **Daily Check-in** | "Good morning! You got 2 new reviews overnight. Reply?" | +15% daily engagement |
| **Milestone Moments** | "100 reviews milestone! Here's your badge for your website." | Emotional connection |
| **Competitive Insight** | "You're outperforming 67% of cafes in your area this week." | Curiosity + motivation |
| **Streak Tracker** | "You've posted consistently for 4 weeks. Keep it going!" | Habit formation |

### 2. Dash (WordPress Command Palette)

**Existing Hooks:**
- ✅ Recent items (returns you to where you were)
- ✅ Efficiency gain (time savings feel good)

**Missing Hooks:**
- ❌ No engagement tracking ("You saved 47 minutes this week")
- ❌ No progressive mastery
- ❌ No personalization over time
- ❌ No community/shared learning

**Retention Gap:** Dash is a utility, not a habit. Users appreciate it when they use it but don't think about it otherwise.

**Recommendations:**

| Hook | Implementation | Expected Impact |
|------|----------------|-----------------|
| **Weekly Stats** | "You used Dash 127 times this week, saving ~32 minutes." | Value reinforcement |
| **Command Mastery** | "You've discovered 12 of 24 commands. Try '> cache' next." | Progressive disclosure |
| **Team Leaderboard** | "Your team's top Dash user this week: @sarah" | Social competition |
| **Pro Tips** | "Tip: Type '@ + username' to find any team member instantly." | Ongoing education |

### 3. Pinned (WordPress Sticky Notes)

**Existing Hooks:**
- ✅ @mention notifications (pulls you back when mentioned)
- ✅ Acknowledgment tracking (social proof)
- ✅ Visual aging (creates urgency as notes get stale)
- ✅ Auto-expiration (FOMO—notes disappear)

**This is actually your strongest retention product.** The mechanics are there.

**Missing Hooks:**
- ❌ No digest of activity ("This week on your Pinned board...")
- ❌ No celebration of team engagement
- ❌ No historical value ("Your most common note topics")

**Recommendations:**

| Hook | Implementation | Expected Impact |
|------|----------------|-----------------|
| **Weekly Team Summary** | "5 notes created, 12 acknowledgments, 3 expired." | Ritual + value proof |
| **Engagement Celebration** | "Your team hit 100% acknowledgment rate this week!" | Team motivation |
| **Pattern Insight** | "Your most-noted topics: Deadlines, Client requests" | Institutional value |

**Retention Score: 7/10** — Strongest in portfolio, needs minor polish.

**Missing Hooks:**
- ❌ No "ongoing story" for the user
- ❌ No progress visualization
- ❌ No community connection
- ❌ No achievement system

**Retention Gap:** Users engage during projects but disconnect between them. The learning happens invisibly.

**Recommendations:**

| Hook | Implementation | Expected Impact |
|------|----------------|-----------------|
| **Agency Story** | "Your agency has shipped 5 products, learned 155 patterns, and avoided 8 known bugs." | Emotional investment |
| **Learning Feed** | "New learning acquired: 'Always verify API endpoints before UI build'" | Visible growth |

| **Memory Digest** | Weekly email: "What your agency learned this week" | Engagement between projects |

**The Big Opportunity:** Great Minds should feel like a **growing partner**, not a tool you use occasionally. Every learning should be visible. Every improvement should be celebrated.

## Cross-Portfolio Retention Patterns

### What's Working
1. **Pinned** has the best retention mechanics (notifications, social proof, expiration)
2. **Great Minds** has hidden retention value (memory accumulation) that needs surfacing
3. **LocalGenius** has weekly digest (good start, needs more)

### What's Broken
1. **Shipyard** has zero retention—purely transactional
2. **Dash** is invisible between uses—no engagement hooks
3. **No product sends a "we miss you" re-engagement message**

### Universal Recommendations

**1. Weekly Digest for Every Product**
Every product should send a weekly summary of value delivered. This is table stakes.

**2. Milestone Celebrations**
- 10th command used (Dash)
- 100th review (LocalGenius)
- 10th note acknowledged (Pinned)
- 5th project shipped (Great Minds)
- 1 year anniversary (Shipyard)

**3. Progress Visualization**
Users should see their journey. Before/after. Growth over time. "You've come so far."

**4. Re-engagement Triggers**
If a user hasn't engaged in 7 days, reach out. "We noticed you haven't checked your dashboard. Here's what you missed."

## The Deeper Truth

**Products that retain users tell a story.**

Every great show has:
- A protagonist (your user)
- A journey (their progress)
- Stakes (what they might lose)
- Celebration (when they win)

Right now, your products are **episodes without a series arc**. Users complete a task and leave. There's no "previously on..." to pull them back.

**The fix:** Make every product feel like Chapter 1 of a longer story. Great Minds especially—it's literally learning and growing. Show that growth. Celebrate that growth. Make users feel like they're building something that compounds.

People don't come back for features. **They come back for their story.**
