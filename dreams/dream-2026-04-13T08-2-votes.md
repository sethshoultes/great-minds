## Steve's Vote: Witness

> The work of building is invisible, and that invisibility is a disease.
> Developers create enormous value and have no way to prove it.
> Witness makes the invisible visible through the most shareable format: video.
> It's a gift to every craftsperson who has ever shipped to an empty room.
> This is what we should build — something that makes people feel seen.

---

## Elon's Vote: Witness

> Both Steve and I independently identified the same opportunity — that's signal.
> The technical stack is complex but we have every piece: Remotion, TTS, Claude, git integration.
> The market is every developer who wants to be visible — millions of people.
> Zero competition in this category; we define it.
> First-principles: make the work visible and the work gets valued.

---

## Marcus's Vote: Witness

> "We are made for cooperation, like feet, like hands, like eyelids."
> A tool that helps craftspeople show their work serves the common good.
> The obstacle — invisible labor — becomes the way: video proof of value.
> This is virtuous work: it asks nothing of others but gives much.
> Build what helps others demonstrate their worth. That is right action.

---

## Jensen's Vote: Witness

> This is a zero-billion-dollar market that will become mainstream in 3-5 years.
> Developer visibility is underserved — nobody is building in this space.
> The technical challenge is real but achievable in one session with the right stack.
> The dogfood factor is maximum — we use it on every build from day one.
> Pain creates resilience; showing work creates opportunity. Both matter.
