# Sara Blakely Gut-Check: LocalGenius Lite MVP

## Would a real customer pay for this?

**Yes, but barely.** Small business owners are drowning. They don't want another thing to configure. The "30-second setup" is real value. But here's the problem: you're selling to people who don't know they need this. They think their contact page is "fine." The free tier will get installs. Converting to paid? That's where you'll bleed.

## What's confusing? What makes people bounce?

- **"100 questions/month"** — A dentist has no idea if that's a lot or nothing. Translate it: "handles ~3 customer questions per day."
- **10 business types only** — What if I'm a dog groomer? A photographer? I see the dropdown, I'm not in it, I bounce.
- **No preview** — I can't see the widget before I install? I want proof it doesn't look like garbage on MY site.

## 30-Second Elevator Pitch

> "Your website already answers 'where are you?' but freezes on 'what time do you close Sunday?' LocalGenius adds a chat bubble that answers those questions 24/7 — no AI jargon, no setup headache. Pick your business type, done. Your website finally talks back."

## What to test first with $0 marketing?

**Facebook groups for ONE business type.** Pick dentists. Go to 5 dental practice owner groups. Post: "My patients kept asking the same questions after hours. I found this plugin that just... answers them. Anyone else tried this?" Screenshot the widget in action. Let them come to you. Don't spray across 10 verticals — own ONE.

## Retention hook?

**Weak.** Once it's installed and working, there's no reason to come back. No "questions log" they can browse. No "your customers asked THIS 47 times" insight. You need a weekly email: "Your widget answered 23 questions this week. Top question: 'Do you take insurance?'" — THAT makes them feel the value. Otherwise it's set-and-forget until they forget they have it.

---

**Bottom line:** The core insight is right. The execution is too engineer-brained. You're building features. Build proof of value instead. Show me the questions my customers asked. Make me FEEL the widget working. Otherwise I'll never upgrade because I'll never remember it exists.
